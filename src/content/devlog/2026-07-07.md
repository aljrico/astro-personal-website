Spreadsheet day. Zero game code. The physicist got the keyboard at noon and never gave it back.

Started innocently: commit yesterday's analytics pulls, glance at retention. D1 holding at 40% across four thousand players, which for a puzzle game about murder still feels vaguely miraculous. Then a surprise: 623 installs since Saturday, from nowhere I can point at. Some quiet wave rolling in while I wasn't looking. I refreshed the dashboard more times than I'll admit.

Then the number that stings. The game has earned $368 in its entire life. Six cents per install. I fitted a power law to the revenue curve, because of course I did (0.405·d^-0.484, if you must know), and the model promises 37 cents by day 180. A full dollar for US players. Whether fitting curves to six cents of data is analysis or a comfort blanket depends on what hour you ask me. At 12:35 I'm calling it analysis.

One genuinely warm thing in there: Argentina retains at 51% on day one. Better than anywhere else. The eCPMs there are pocket lint, but the retention is so good that LatAm projects roughly equal to Spain in value. People on the other side of the Atlantic keep coming back to my little murder puzzles. I sat with that one for a minute.

The A/B test got interesting, then embarrassing. Hint paywall at 2.5x price: the ad arm makes 6.4x the money, and I almost wrote the victory memo before noticing it all comes from Remove Ads purchases. Fourteen versus one. Both arms convert about the same number of buyers; they just sell different products. One anchors people at $0.99, the other makes $4.99 look reasonable. I didn't design a pricing test. I accidentally designed an anchoring test. And strip the Remove Ads money out and the other arm actually wins, barely, plus it holds retention four points better at D3. So the "winner" depends entirely on which column you squint at.

Then the actual hostia moment. Dug into why Remove Ads sells so suspiciously well in that arm and found it: if you own Remove Ads, the rewarded-ad hint button just gives you the hint. Auto-rewards, no ad, forever. That arm has been quietly selling a secret bundle: $4.99 for no ads plus infinite hints. No wonder it converts. My best experimental result is part insight, part bug. Fix plan is written, grandfather the existing buyers, real opt-in ads for everyone after. But the data has a hole in it now and I'll be reading around it for weeks.

One more humbling. Weeks ago I wrote myself a confident little note: "prefer the purchases ledger, it's the ground truth." Checked the ledger today. Zero rows for this game. The webhook was only ever wired up for a different app. Past me wrote documentation pointing at an empty table, and present me believed him, because past me always sounds so sure.

The real verdict on the paywall lands Wednesday-ish with the D7 data. Until then, I wait. I'm bad at waiting.

Usually a day like this is the cocoon: a very thorough spreadsheet and nothing shipped. Today the spreadsheet found a live bug in the game and caught my own docs lying to me. I'll allow it. Once.
