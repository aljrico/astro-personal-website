Analytics day. Zero product code. Spent the whole session being a forensic accountant of my own game, and I'd be lying if I said I didn't enjoy it.

First thing in the dashboard: 623 installs since Saturday. No launch, no post, no reason. Just a wave out of nowhere, with eCPM sitting at 4.45€, which for my usual numbers is champagne. Hostia. I kept refreshing like the numbers might apologize and go back down. Somewhere out there people are finding the game and I have no idea how. The ghost audience showing up unannounced, and of course nobody says hi. They just install.

Retention is holding at 40% D1 on mature cohorts, four thousand users deep now. Saturday's cohort dipped to 34.5 and I almost spiraled before remembering weekend cohorts always dip. Then the LTV work: fitted a power law to revenue per install, r(d) = 0.405·d^-0.484, which projects to about $0.37 per install by D180, closer to a dollar in the US. Best finding of the day: Argentina retains 51% at D1, which is absurd, and it makes LatAm worth roughly as much as Spain in projected value despite the worst eCPMs on the planet. Retention beats price. I love when the data says something I wouldn't have guessed.

The A/B test was the detective story.

The hint paywall arm with ads is making 6.4x the revenue of the pure IAP arm. Slam dunk, right? Except no. All of the gap is Remove Ads purchases, 14 versus 1, p around .002. Buyer counts are basically the same in both arms. The arms aren't converting better or worse, they're selling different products: one anchors people at $4.99, the other at $0.99. And the IAP arm quietly wins retention, +4.2 points at D3, and even edges ahead on revenue if you take Remove Ads out of the picture. What looked like a blowout is a coin toss with a plot twist. Verdict deferred to D7 data. Thursday, maybe.

And then the confounder. Joder. Remove Ads owners in the ad arm have been getting infinite free hints this whole time, because rewarded_ad.dart auto-rewards when ads are disabled. I wrote that fallback months ago as a kindness to paying users. It's been leaking free hints into my experiment ever since. My own generosity, sabotaging my own science. Fix plan sketched: grandfather the existing buyers, opt-in rewarded ads for new ones. Plan only. Nothing written.

Bonus humiliation: the purchases ledger I told myself to "prefer" as the source of truth has zero rows for this game. The webhook was only ever wired up for a different app. Past me wrote a confident note pointing at an empty table. Corrected the README. Past me is usually the genius in these pages; today he was the guy who recommended a database without checking it had data in it.

Also noticed the duel rollout dial sits at 100%, which means no control group, which means I'll never get a clean causal read on the biggest feature I've shipped this year. Wrote myself a note to hold 10% back. The physicist winced. The artist shrugged. They're still negotiating.

Closed the laptop and realized the day produced exactly zero verdicts. The retention dip needs D7. The paywall test needs D7. The install wave needs more days to mean anything. Analytics is farming: you plant cohorts and you wait. I did good work today and I can't prove any of it until Thursday.
