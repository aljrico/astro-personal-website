Not the game today. The guide-chunking pipeline.

Built a chunker for three markdown guides — legal study material, àmbits A through C, twenty topics total. 289 chunks out the other end. Clean, namespaced, ready to feed. The kind of plumbing work that feels invisible but holds everything together.

The real hit was the prompt fix. Had a LENGTH_RANK_TARGET compliance problem — the model was ignoring the output format rules, coming in at 13%. Terse bullet-point instructions. The kind of thing you write when you're being clever instead of being clear. Replaced rules 8 and 9 with step-by-step instructions, the way you'd explain it to someone who's smart but literal-minded. Which is exactly what a language model is.

13% to 90%. One rewrite.

There's something deeply satisfying about prompt engineering when it clicks. It's not coding. It's not writing. It's somewhere in between — closer to tuning an instrument than building one. You're not changing what the model *can* do, you're changing what it *does* do. The gap between those two things is where all the craft lives.

Added a `--tag` flag to the question generator so guide batches and legal doc batches stay in their own lanes. Separate JSONL outputs, no clobbering. Small decision, saves future headaches. The kind of thing you only think to add after you've been burned once.

Submitted 286 chunks to gpt-5.2. Seventy-six cents. Hostia. The economics of this still catch me off guard sometimes. A year ago this batch would've cost ten times that and taken twice as long. Now it's a coffee. Less than a coffee.

Batch is running. Tomorrow I'll know if the compliance fix holds at scale or if I was just lucky on the test set. Probably holds. The instructions are unambiguous now. But "probably" is doing a lot of work in that sentence.

Not a flashy day. Pipeline days never are. But there's a quiet satisfaction in watching chunks flow through cleanly, in seeing a number jump from 13 to 90, in knowing the next batch will just work. The plumbing is the project. Everything else is decoration.
