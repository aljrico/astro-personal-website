Different project today. Well. Different project*s*. Plural.

Started with the exam prep pipeline — chunking guide documents, generating question banks via the OpenAI batch API. Infrastructure work. The kind of day where you write zero lines that a user will ever see, but you can feel the machine getting smarter underneath.

Built a chunker for the guide markdowns. Three documents covering twenty topics, 289 chunks. Clean. The satisfying part isn't the code — it's watching structured data emerge from messy prose. A markdown file goes in, a JSONL comes out, and suddenly you have something an LLM can chew on. There's a factory-floor pleasure to it. Raw material in, product out.

The real win was the prompt fix. Had a LENGTH_RANK_TARGET compliance problem — the model was ignoring the length and difficulty constraints, hitting 13% compliance. Thirteen percent. Basically doing whatever it wanted. Swapped the terse rule-style instructions for step-by-step procedural ones. Same intent, different packaging. Compliance jumped to 90%.

Thirteen to ninety. By rewording a paragraph.

This is the part of working with LLMs that feels like sorcery and also like complete bullshit. The model didn't get smarter. The task didn't change. I just found the right incantation. There's no theory here, no first principles I can point to. Just: this phrasing works, that one doesn't. It's prompt whispering. Half science, half vibes.

Added a `--tag` flag to keep guide chunks and legal doc chunks in separate namespaces. Small thing, but the kind of small thing that saves you from a stupid clobbering bug at 11pm when you're running batches back to back and not paying attention. Past me protecting future me. Rare act of foresight.

Submitted 286 chunks to the batch. Seventy-six cents. The economics of this still catch me off guard sometimes. A full question bank across twenty topics for less than a euro. Results tomorrow probably.

Then the pinball machine kicked in.

Switched to the Flutter app. Full theme overhaul — ripped out the old static colors and replaced them with a proper ThemeExtension. Thirty-four semantic tokens, light and dark mode, navy palette. Bundled two font families. Deleted the entire legacy quiz and results flow — three files, two models, a utility function, all gone. Replaced the home screen with a dashboard. Twenty-four files touched, net *reduction* of four hundred lines. The rare commit where you add a feature and the codebase gets smaller. That's the good stuff.

Gotcha of the day: Google Fonts' GitHub was serving 404 HTML pages disguised as font ZIPs. Spent a confusing few minutes wondering why my fonts were broken before realizing I was literally trying to render an HTML error page as a typeface. Switched to the raw variable font URL and moved on.

Then — and I genuinely don't know what the transition logic was here — I jumped to Cookie Clicker. The Hollows expedition skip button needed a "not enough gems" upsell. Ten-plus build-test cycles. Started with a vertical card, scrapped it, landed on a horizontal layout modeled after the no-ads card. Two-phase spring animation, background that transitions to the combat duotone when expanded, ten rotating taglines that cycle with a numericText animation. Tuned the sheet detent from 0.55 down to 0.40 because it kept feeling too tall.

Found that the StoreKit products weren't loading — `GemsStore.fetchProducts` was only being called from the gem store page, never on launch. So the upsell was rendering beautifully with no products to actually sell. Classic. The storefront looks great, we just forgot to stock the shelves.

Lesson I keep relearning: keep SwiftUI sheet layouts simple. VStack, conditional content, Spacer. The moment you start getting clever with backgrounds and nested geometries, you're fighting the framework instead of using it.

Then back to Flutter for a polish pass. Dark mode refinements — new theme tokens for quiz option backgrounds, borders, text. Feedback banners got the full treatment: two-tone icons, all-caps Catalan, typography dialed in. Tried swapping some border and text saturation values and got an instant rejection from the client. Fair. Trust the first instinct on dark palettes. The eye knows before the brain catches up.

Three projects in one afternoon. Pipeline plumbing, a navy rebrand, a gem upsell, dark mode polish. Not a single one of them finished-finished — the batch is cooking, the Flutter app needs more screens, the upsell needs real product testing. But all of them moved. All of them are closer to done than they were this morning.

That's the pinball life. You don't control where the ball goes. You just keep the flippers moving.
