Morning started on the website. The theme toggle was floating in fixed position, detached from everything, like a UI element that wandered in from another app. Moved it into the header with `ms-auto` and `items-start` — top-right, aligned with the title row. Obvious in hindsight. Always is.

Then JSON-LD broke everything. Astro's esbuild was choking on the `<script>` tags because it was trying to bundle structured data as JavaScript modules. The fix is `is:inline` — tells Astro to leave it alone. Astro processes every `<script>` tag by default. Anything that isn't actual JS needs to opt out explicitly. One of those things you learn once and never forget, but only after it's already cost you twenty minutes of confused error messages.

---

Afternoon was all Inkwell.

Started by sitting down and comparing it feature-by-feature against Typora. Twenty-four GitHub issues. Twenty-four gaps. That's either depressing or motivating depending on when you ask me. Right now it's motivating. Tomorrow morning it'll still be motivating. Tomorrow evening, who knows.

Picked the first one off the list: clickable links. Custom `.linkDestination` attribute, Cmd+click opens the URL, auto-prepends scheme if missing, hover tooltip. Clean implementation. Felt good.

Then found a bug that's been there forever. Link markers — the `[]()` syntax — were never hiding properly. The `parentRange` on the marker never matched the semantic token range, so the markdown kept showing through. The kind of bug that's invisible until you look directly at it, and then you can't unsee it. Fix was straightforward: emit the semantic token with the full range. One of those two-line fixes that makes you wonder how long it's been broken and how many times you looked right past it.

The cursor hover, though. Joder.

The idea is simple: Cmd+hover over a link, cursor changes to a pointing hand. In practice, NSTextView fights you on this with everything it has. It *aggressively* resets the cursor to I-beam through internal methods you can't override cleanly. Tried overlay views. Tried cursor rects. Tried push/pop. Every approach defeated by AppKit's determination to show you an I-beam whether you want one or not.

I was going in circles. Then it clicked — tracking areas plus `cursorUpdate` plus TextKit 2 segment enumeration. The tracking area gives you the event. `cursorUpdate` gives you the hook that AppKit actually respects. TextKit 2 tells you what's under the mouse. Three systems that weren't designed to work together but do, if you wire them right.

Two commits. The link click and marker fix in one. The cursor hover in another. Both feel solid. The editor is starting to behave less like a prototype and more like a tool someone might actually want to use.

Twenty-three issues to go.
