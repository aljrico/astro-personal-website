Ten exponential discount formulas. Ten places where the math said "sure, keep stacking revelations, the discount will approach zero but never quite—" except it did. Past 250 revelation levels the grandma-temple-kraken cascade was driving modifiers into the dirt. Not asymptotically approaching zero. Actually zero. The kind of bug that only shows up when someone plays your game way more than you expected anyone would.

The fix is hyperbolic: `1/(1+k*n*f(x))`. Same first-order feel — the initial discounts hit the same, the early game doesn't notice the change. But now the curve flattens instead of cratering. I derived the coefficients from `-c*ln(base)` to match the original discount slopes at low levels. Physicist brain got to play for an hour. The spreadsheet was unnecessary but satisfying.

Buffed Veritaserum 10x while I was in there. And Coriolis Sickness. And fixed a label that said 1% when it was actually 5%. The Cornish Pixies have been lying to players for who knows how long. Nobody noticed. Nobody ever notices the tooltips. I notice, and it bothers me, and that's enough.

Shipped Build 4 to TestFlight. Wrote the v5.3 player-facing devlog. Reviewed the full 5.4 diff. The responsible stuff that feels like homework but isn't.

---

The afternoon was cleanup. Ripped out the old daily rewards overlay — the floating button, the full-screen page, the settings toggle. All of it. The rewards live in the gems store now, one card among others, no drama. Tabs went from four to three. `isDailyRewardsHidden` got purged from storage keys and settings.

This code was already dead. I'd done the work outside the session, just reviewed and committed here. But there's something satisfying about deleting UI. Adding features is exciting. Removing them is *clarifying*. Three tabs instead of four. One less button competing for attention. The screen breathes a little better.

Some days are math. Some days are subtraction. Today was both. Neither felt like a breakthrough. Both felt right.
