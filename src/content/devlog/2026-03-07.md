Found the exploit by accident. Revelations past 250 were driving building modifiers to zero — not approaching zero, actually hitting it. Exponential discounts cascading across buildings. Grandma feeds into temple feeds into kraken, and somewhere around level 300 the whole economy collapses into free everything forever.

The physicist brain kicked in before I could stop it. Spent the morning sketching curves. Exponential decay is elegant until it isn't — it crosses zero too fast when you stack multipliers. Hyperbolic form: `1/(1 + k·n·f(x))`. Asymptotic approach. The modifier *wants* to reach zero but mathematically can't. Which is exactly the fantasy you want in an idle game — the feeling of infinite power that's actually bounded.

The trick was matching the feel. Players at revelation level 10 shouldn't notice anything changed. Derived coefficients from `-c·ln(base)` to preserve first-order behavior. Ten formulas rewritten. Same early game, sane late game. The kind of fix where you can't tell anything happened unless you're the person who broke it in the first place.

Buffed Veritaserum 10x while I was in there. The cauldron needed it. Coriolis Sickness too. Fixed the Cornish Pixies label that said 1% but was actually doing 5%. Nobody reported it. Either nobody reads tooltips or nobody cares. Both options are depressing in their own way.

Shipped Build 4 to TestFlight. Wrote the v5.3 player-facing devlog. Reviewed the full 5.4 diff. The ritual of reading your own branch end-to-end — it's either reassuring or terrifying and today it was reassuring.

Then the cleanup. Ripped out the old daily rewards overlay — the floating button, the full-screen page, the settings toggle. All of it. The rewards live in the gems store card now. One surface instead of three. Tabs went from four to three. Code deleted is code you never have to debug again.

There's a specific pleasure in deleting UI that you built yourself. No sentimentality. The old version served its purpose and now it's gone. `isDailyRewardsHidden` — removed from storage keys, removed from settings. A feature that needed a hide toggle was already confessing it didn't belong where it was.

Two very different kinds of work today. The morning was math — careful, analytical, the kind of thing where you check your derivatives twice. The afternoon was demolition — fast, satisfying, irreversible. Both felt productive. Both shipped. That's a good day by any measure.
