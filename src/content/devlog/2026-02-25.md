Two projects today. Two completely different energies.

Started on FlamaCoach — the guide topic descriptions needed real text instead of raw headings. Built a batch script, pointed gpt-5.2 at the twenty topics, got back two-sentence summaries. Looked at them on the phone. Too wordy. The cards couldn't breathe.

So I tightened the prompt. "UNA SOLA FRASE, màxim 20 paraules, estil telegràfic." Ran it again. Twelve cents total for both batches. The results came back as keyword soup. Technically correct. Completely soulless.

Third iteration: I wrote them all by hand.

Twenty descriptions. One by one. Found a voice on the first one — "Evolució política i social de Catalunya des de l'antiguitat fins al segle XVIII: romanització, feudalisme, expansió mediterrània i guerres" — and knew immediately that's what they all needed. A framing clause, a colon, then the key milestones. Rhythm matters. These are the first thing a student reads when they open a topic. The LLM could approximate the format but it couldn't feel the weight of the words.

The lesson is clean: at a hundred items, batch wins. At twenty items where tone matters, you sit down and write. The batch script stays in the repo for whenever the question bank scales past what hands can hold. But today, hands were better.

There's something funny about spending an hour building a pipeline, running it twice, then throwing out the output and doing it yourself. The physicist in me wants to call that waste. The rest of me knows the pipeline taught me what "good" looked like. I needed the bad versions to recognize the good one.

Documented the whole rebuild pipeline while the decisions were still warm. That's a lesson I keep relearning — documentation written at 7pm on the day you made the choices is worth ten times the archaeology you'd do three months later trying to remember why you picked JSONL over CSV.

---

Then I switched to Hollows and the energy shifted completely.

Shipped phases 3-6 in one commit. Toll nodes, shrine nodes, sanity drain, mimics. The meat of the strata system, the stuff that makes the dungeon feel dangerous instead of decorative. Tolls cost coins or ore. Shrines trade fangs for sanity. Sanity drain halves what you've got. Mimics pretend to be chests then bite.

The icon swap was the highlight. Replaced all the SF Symbols with the Rhos fantasy pack — those iCloud assets I've been sitting on. The boss node got a breathing purple glow at 1.5x size. Tried a King icon first. Wrong. Too regal, too clean. The Eye silhouette hit immediately — Lovecraftian, watching, *wrong* in the right way. That's the aesthetic. Not fantasy-noble. Fantasy-unsettling.

This is the stuff that puts me in flow. Not the architecture, not the data models. The moment the screen looks like the thing in my head. I kept triggering the boss glow just to watch it pulse. Stupid. Satisfying.

Two projects, two modes. FlamaCoach was careful, iterative, humbling — the machine couldn't do what I needed, so I did it myself. Hollows was fast, instinctive, visual — four phases shipped in one burst because the momentum was there and I didn't stop to think about whether the code was clean.

Both valid. Both real. The spreadsheet and the brushstroke, same afternoon.
