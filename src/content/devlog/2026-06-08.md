Easy one today. Or it was supposed to be.

Settings needs a "leave us a review" row. Permanent one — a button that's always there, not the rate-limited `in_app_review` popup that Apple lets you fire three times a year and then just silently ignores. Two different things. The popup is a slot machine. This is a door you can always open.

So I reach for `in_app_review`, obviously. It's right there. It has an `openStoreListing`. Done by lunch.

Except I asked Codex first, mostly out of habit, and it talked me out of it. On Android `openStoreListing` fires a generic `ACTION_VIEW` that can land in a browser, or worse, a chooser dialog — "open with…" and now the user is picking between Chrome and the Play Store to give me five stars they were never going to give me anyway. The `market://` deep link is just more reliable. Direct. iOS gets the `action=write-review` URL that drops you straight on the rating screen, no detour.

So the "easy" library was the wrong call and the slightly-more-manual thing was the right one. I like when that happens. Feels like the universe confirming that the path of least resistance is usually least for a reason.

Skipped the `canLaunchUrl` preflight on purpose. It lies — false negatives all over the place, and to make it tell the truth you have to declare every URL scheme in Info.plist and an Android `<queries>` block, and now I'm maintaining a list of schemes to make a function work that I didn't want in the first place. Just launch it. Catch the failure. Fall back to the https URL. SnackBar if even that dies. Three layers, no manifest surgery. Code quality people would hate it. It works.

The part I actually enjoyed: the icons. The settings rows are on SVGs now — translate, help, star — running through a shared duotone tile. And *that's* where the time went, of course. Not the launch logic, not the fallback chain. The icons. Getting the muted color right, the second tone sitting underneath the first. That's the bit my hands wanted to keep doing. I'd have the whole review flow working and still be nudging the star's fill by a shade nobody will ever consciously notice.

`flutter analyze` is clean. Not device-tested yet, which means it doesn't really work, it just *compiles*. There's a difference and I know it and I'm choosing to feel good anyway.

The "How to Play" tile is still sitting there with an empty `onTap`. A stub. A little WIP ghost I walked right past again. It's been there a while. It'll be there tomorrow. Nothing's abandoned. It's just postponed.

Good morning of work though. Wrong-library-into-right-answer, two icons I keep looking at, a button that points people at a review screen for an app almost nobody's downloaded yet.

I'll think about that second part later. Right now I'm just enjoying the star.

Afternoon. The Hint button.

I'd been dreading this one all week without admitting it. The plan was to restyle Hint and slap an "Ad" badge on it, and in my head that meant ad plumbing — rewarded controllers, callbacks, the whole tangle. A big change wearing the costume of a small one.

Then I actually looked. The Hint was *already* rewarded-ad gated. `_useHint` already calls the rewarded controller's `show`. It's been doing that the whole time. So the "Ad" badge isn't a promise of work I haven't done — it's just telling the truth that was already sitting there, undeclared. The scary big change collapsed into a pure restyle. Joder, what a relief. Half the dread in this job is the gap between how big a thing looks before you open the file and how big it actually is.

The restyle itself was almost free. Dropped Hint onto the shared `GameButton` base constructor, and its default proportions happened to line up exactly with the cookie-clicker shuffle button I ported a while back. The colors were already in `AppColors` from that same port — caribbean green, persian green, orient. Square, 48px, white icon. Sometimes past-me leaves the table set and you just sit down.

The badge is its own little widget now — `AdBadge`. White rounded pill, "Ad" in orient, a hairline border. Overlaid top-right with a `Stack`, `clipBehavior: Clip.none`, nudged just past the corner so it bites the edge. I keep looking at it. Same disease as the morning. A tiny pill advertising ads on an app barely anyone has installed. There's something funny and slightly sad about that and I'm not going to poke at it too hard.

And then the icon fought me, because of course it did.

Copied a lightbulb SVG out of the icon pack. Rendered as a solid black square. No lightbulb. Just a black box where my hint should be. Spent longer than I'd like staring at it before I opened the source: the thing fills its shapes through a CSS `<style>` block — `.cls-2{fill:#fff}` for the visible white — and there's a full-bleed background rect hidden *only* by `.cls-1{opacity:0}`. flutter_svg doesn't do CSS. So it ignores the opacity rule, the rect comes back from the dead, and it's black. The invisible thing made visible. Rewrote the SVG with inline `fill="#ffffff"`, killed the bounding rect, done. First I'd grabbed "Lightbulb" lit; switched to "Lightbulb Off" because an unused hint shouldn't already be glowing.

The slow part wasn't even the bug, it was the loop. flutter_svg caches its rasterized pictures, so hot reload just shrugs at a changed asset. Every tweak meant kill the sim, relaunch, wait. iPhone 17 sim, over and over. One "Lost connection to device" out of nowhere for flavor. You forget how much of this is just waiting for a rectangle to redraw.

Committed and pushed. That part feels good — clean, on master, real.

What's *not* committed: the l10n changes and an untracked `.serena/` folder, still sitting in the working tree. Another ghost. The morning had its "How to Play" stub, the afternoon has these. I walk past both. I tell myself tomorrow. We'll see if tomorrow agrees.

But the button looks right. The badge bites the corner just so. And after a week of quietly fearing this, it turned out to be an afternoon. I'll take the trade.
