Small day. One CTA row in the settings sheet. "Leave us a review." A star icon, a deep link, done.

Except it was never going to be that simple, because the obvious move was `in_app_review` — the native popup, the one Apple and Google bless. And I almost reached for it. Then I asked Codex to sanity-check me and it talked me right back out of it. The Android side of that package fires a generic `ACTION_VIEW` that can dump the user into a browser, or worse, a chooser dialog. "Which app would you like to use to open this Play Store link?" Nobody wants that. So `market://` it is, more reliable, with an https fallback for the edge cases.

The funny part: the in-app popup is rate-limited anyway. The OS decides if it even shows. So a permanent row in settings isn't redundant — it's the thing that actually works whenever someone genuinely wants to leave a review. The popup is a slot machine. The settings row is a door that's always open.

iOS gets the nicer deal — `action=write-review` lands you straight on the write screen. Android has no sanctioned write-review deep link at all, so the best I can do is drop you on the listing and trust you to find the stars. Joder. Always Android.

The thing I'm quietly proud of: I skipped the `canLaunchUrl` preflight. It throws false negatives constantly, and the moment you call it you inherit a whole tax — `LSApplicationQueriesSchemes` in the Info.plist on iOS, a `<queries>` block on Android, all to ask permission to ask a question. So instead: just launch, catch, fall back, and if everything fails, a SnackBar that says sorry. Less code, fewer manifest incantations, and it degrades gracefully. Code quality is overrated; this is just *correct* and smaller at the same time.

While I was in there I put the settings rows on proper SVG icons — translate, help, star — through a shared `_ActionTile` with a duotone muted palette. That's the part where I actually had fun. Watching the three icons line up in their little muted blues, consistent. That's the flow state, the visual click. Not the URL plumbing. The plumbing is just plumbing.

`flutter analyze` is clean. Not device-tested yet, which means it's not really done, it just looks done. And the "How to Play" tile is still sitting there with an empty `onTap`, a stub mocking me every time I scroll past it. One thing finished, one thing pointedly not.

That's the whole day. A door that's always open, and a button next to it that still does nothing.
