Apple bounced 5.18 for guideline 3.1.1 again. This time the complaint was: you sell permanent stuff, but there's no visible way to restore it. Fair, honestly. The restore *worked* — it just happened invisibly through iCloud sync, which to a reviewer looks identical to not existing.

They suggested account registration. Absolutely not. I'm not bolting a login system onto a cookie clicker to satisfy a checkbox. Instead: a Restore Purchases button in Settings. Tap it, it runs the StoreKit sync, refreshes transactions, pulls cloud state. Spinner for at least a second even if it finishes instantly, then a checkmark. The one-second minimum is pure theater — if the button responds instantly, people assume it did nothing. Sometimes UX is lying politely.

The interesting part of the day wasn't the button. It was realizing I'd been carrying a wrong mental model of my own cloud sync for who knows how long. I'd have told you, confidently, that reconciliation was last-write-wins on timestamps. It's not. It's a lexicographic compare down a chain of monotonic counters — total IAPs first, then permanent boosts, islands, ascensions, wealth, on down — and the timestamp only breaks ties. Skins are union-merged so they can never shrink. Past me built something smarter than present me remembered. There's that feeling again: reading old code and wondering what ruined me.

And while I was in there I found a real gap. Individual permanent-boost ownership flags don't get union-merged the way skins do. Buy a boost offline on one device while the other device wins reconciliation, and the purchase can evaporate. Rare. Real. I left it open, deliberately — today was about shipping the review fix, not about fixing every ghost in the sync layer. Wrote it down so it haunts a future session instead of this one.

Built it, deployed to the phone, and then the launch failed because the phone was locked. Sitting right next to me. The whole pipeline — code, build, install, launch — automated end to end, defeated by a lock screen. Venga.

5.19 is off to review. Third round with this guideline. The button exists, it's visible, it spins. Your move, Apple.
