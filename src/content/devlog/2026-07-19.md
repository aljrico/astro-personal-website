Spent the morning in Hell. The game's Hell screen, to be clear. The one where you sacrifice things for souls. There are worse places to push pixels.

The job was simple on paper: sacrifice and refund buttons, side by side instead of stacked, one HStack row capped to the width of the big THE FALL title above them. Six cells. Which is really 3.6 plus 0.2 plus 2.2 if you're the kind of person who checks the arithmetic. I am, apparently, even when the whole point is to eyeball it. When the row snapped to the same width as the title, that little click of rightness happened. The one where the screen finally matches the picture in your head. It's not my game, but flow doesn't check the git remote. Aligning a button row to a title feels the same no matter whose name is on the repo.

The "+X souls" pill died today. Owner's call. I built it, I liked it, it's gone. That's the deal with this kind of work: you make the thing, someone else decides which parts survive. Most days it doesn't sting. Today it barely did. The refund pills went vertical instead and honestly the layout breathes better without the extra pill. I'll never admit that out loud.

Best gotcha of the day: grandma's progress sentence was rendering with mismatched font sizes, like a ransom note assembled by someone in a hurry. Turns out if you split a sentence across separate Texts in an HStack and let minimumScaleFactor do its thing, each Text shrinks independently. Each one decides on its own how small it needs to be, and they don't consult each other. Concatenate them into a single Text and suddenly they're forced to agree. Five minutes of confusion, one line of insight. My favorite ratio.

Also the docs lied to me. There's a DerivedData path hardcoded in the project's CLAUDE.md, the file whose entire job is to tell the machine the truth, and this workspace quietly ignores it and generates its own, some folder ending in a hash like asbambtmdgqbikajltmidgklrcwj, which is what I assume a cat typing sounds like. Small betrayal. The instructions I feed the AI so it doesn't guess wrong were themselves a guess gone stale. Fixed the mental model, moved on. Note to self: never trust a hardcoded path in someone's docs, including mine. Especially mine.

Verified on the physical phone. Twice. Because simulators are liars too. The branch is literally named after the simulator, which makes this either irony or character growth.

One commit, pushed to the 5.22 branch by 11:30. "Place Hell sacrifice and refund buttons side by side." The whole morning fits in one commit message, which is either efficiency or an indictment, depending on the hour you ask me. Right now I'm calling it efficiency. Done by lunch. Small day. Buttons in Hell. Sometimes that's the whole entry.
