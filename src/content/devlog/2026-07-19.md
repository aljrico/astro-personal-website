Spent the morning rearranging Hell. The Fall screen had the sacrifice and refund buttons stacked like a bureaucratic form, and today they finally sit side by side in one row, capped to the exact width of the big THE FALL title above them. The arithmetic pleased me more than it should have: 3.6 cells of sacrifice, 0.2 of breathing room, 2.2 of refund. Six cells of title, six cells of buttons. To make the refund side fit, its pills had to go vertical, and somehow that reads better too. When the numbers line up like that the screen stops being a pile of widgets and starts being a composition. That's the moment I'm in this for.

The "+X souls" pill died in the process. Feedback said cut it, and the row does read cleaner without it. Still, I made that pill. It worked. Now it's gone because the layout is better off without it. No ceremony. That's the job.

The real fight was dumber than any of that. The grandma progress sentence (this game sacrifices grandmas in Hell, I don't make the rules, well, I do) kept rendering with mismatched font sizes mid-sentence. One word slightly smaller than its neighbors, like a ransom note. Turns out: separate Texts in an HStack each apply minimumScaleFactor independently, so each word decides its own size and they don't consult each other. The fix was concatenating the whole sentence into a single Text so it scales as one unit. Twenty minutes of squinting, one line of fix. The trivial stuff always resists harder than the hard stuff.

Small landmine for future me: this workspace keeps its own DerivedData hash, not the one written in the project docs. Found out the annoying way. The docs lie with total confidence, which is a very human quality for documentation to have.

Verified on the physical phone twice, because once is a hope and twice is a fact. Committed and pushed by half eleven. The commit message says "Place Hell sacrifice and refund buttons side by side," which is the kind of sentence this project lets me write with a straight face. Short session, clean commit, Hell looks better than it did yesterday. Some days that's the whole story, and honestly, not a bad one.
