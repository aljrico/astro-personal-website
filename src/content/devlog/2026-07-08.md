Polish day. The good kind.

The whole afternoon was the arena screen, and honestly, this is where I live. Not architecture, not clever algorithms. Making the thing look right. Frosted the header into one floating panel — back button, league name, season countdown, trophy rail, all sitting on that soft white glass above the scrolling board. Kept a `flutter run` alive in the background and fired hot reloads at it after every edit. Change, reload, squint, change again. Brushstrokes.

The league name went through the full cycle: 21pt, 25, 30, 40, back to 30. All caps, heavy condensed type, that deep red. For twenty minutes it was too shy, then too shouty, then it clicked. There's no formula for this. You just know when it stops fighting the rest of the screen.

One trap I almost fell into: I wanted the material word — SILVER, GOLD, whatever — at full opacity and the word "League" dimmed, across five languages. First version was a little token classifier, categorizing words, handling connectors. Way too clever. Got called out, replaced the whole thing with one regex that eats the league word and its "de"/"d'" connector. Covers English, Spanish, Portuguese, Catalan, and even the Dutch compound where it's all one word. Falls back to full opacity if it doesn't match. Ten lines instead of a machine. The clever version would have worked too. Nobody would ever have known. That's exactly the problem with clever versions.

Then the zone accents. The promotion and demotion rows had these thick colored borders and they always bothered me — too loud, like the leaderboard was yelling at you. Moved the color into the rank numeral instead. Green number if you're going up, that pink-red if you're going down. Quieter and somehow clearer. Funny moment: asked for a color called "redHeart" that doesn't exist in the palette. I was so sure it did. My own color system, invented by me, and I'm making up names for it like a tourist.

And then the one that made me grin: the senyera. If your device is in Spain but your language is Catalan, the leaderboard flies the Catalan flag instead of the Spanish one. Tiny feature. Handful of lines, a code like a subdivision, an allowlist so old app versions don't crash on a flag they've never heard of. Even the bots mirror it now, so the arena doesn't look like you're the only one. Nobody asked for this. There is no metric that justifies it. Some player in Barcelona is going to open the app, see their flag on the board, and feel *seen*. That's the whole business case. Best commit of the day and it's not close.

Three commits, all visual, all small. No existential crisis today, which almost feels like cheating. Just a screen that looks better tonight than it did this morning, and a flag that shouldn't matter but does.
