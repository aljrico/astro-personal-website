Started the afternoon doing math on gem rewards. The min-of-K distribution had a fat tail I didn't love — upper bound of 67 gems at Tier 8 with K=7. Ran the numbers, pushed K up, flattened the exponent from 1.25 to linear. Cuts P(>=30) by roughly 20x. The physicist brain loves this part. Probability distributions, expected values, knobs with predictable effects. Clean.

Then the tentacles. Three bugs stacked on each other like matryoshka dolls. @State arrays not matching tentacle count during SwiftUI identity changes — index out of range on launch. The pop animation calling `earnCoins` instead of `earn`, which meant squishing a tentacle gave you coins instead of cookies. And the reward display showing a coin icon when it should've been the skin icon. The kind of cascade where you fix one thing and two more fall out. Added bounds guards, `.id()` keying, swapped the earn call. Death wiggle animation looks satisfying now — `popBoost=12.0`, opacity fade, dark green floating number rising up. The small visual things that make a game feel like a game.

The real surgery was SpriteKit. Two stacked full-screen transparent SpriteViews were causing a Metal compositing bug that broke subsequent animations. Traced the root cause across commits — one scene got fixed to always-mount, the other never got the same treatment. Merged CurrencyJuiceScene into what's now EffectsOverlayScene. One animation bus. Then extracted falling cookies from SpriteKit entirely into a UIKit CALayer — render-server driven, zero main-thread cost, correct z-ordering. This is the kind of refactor that doesn't add features but removes an entire class of bugs. Three Codex review rounds caught a z-order regression, a burst FPS tracking gap, missing @MainActor isolation. The AI catching what I missed. The agentic loop working as intended.

Migrated the garden's flying vegetable animations into EffectsOverlayScene too. Had a coordinate conversion saga — tried GridFrame math, scale/offset transforms, GeometryReader. All wrong. The fix was embarrassingly obvious: use SpriteKit's own `convertPoint(toView:)`. Lesson I keep relearning: use the rendering engine's coordinate system, not your approximation of it.

---

Then the AB test results came in and the mood shifted.

`offlineProductionDesign_v1`: killed. Treatment halved noads IAP conversion. Ads revenue ticked up slightly but nowhere near enough to compensate. Dug into version-over-version IAP analysis across 5.2, 5.3, 5.4 and found the real story — the gem sink collapsed. Expedition skip demand dropped 150x. Not a typo. A hundred and fifty times. Because the layer system resets tech tree depth, bosses appear too early, skips are cheap and unnecessary. Nobody needs to spend gems on something that's already easy.

Fix: pushed boss spawn deeper. But the damage to the test is done. Spun up a new test — gem X3 chest boost button at 9 gems with an upsell flow. Wired it into `chestGemBoost_v1` to replace the killed test. The AB testing treadmill. Kill one, birth another. The data doesn't care about your hypothesis.

Found a toll node bug while I was in there. Was checking cookies (`currentWealth`) instead of coins — naming confusion between wealth/coins/cookies that's been a landmine since I added the dual currency. Added a rejection animation: wiggle, red halo, error haptic. If you can't afford the toll, the game should *tell* you, not silently fail.

Tentacle squish sounds were delayed by multiple seconds. SpriteKit's `SKAction.playSoundFileNamed` is tied to the scene update rate, and MainScene idles at 1 FPS. Replaced it with an AVAudioEngine + 4-player pool in SoundEngine.swift. The kind of bug that takes an hour to diagnose and five minutes to fix, and the diagnosis is "the framework does exactly what the docs say, you just didn't read them."

---

Past midnight now. Should've stopped. Didn't.

Rebalanced The Ascent branch. Inheritance moved to slot #1 with +10% earned ascension points. Momentum buffed to +100% production bonus on ascend. Renamed Swift Return to Purpose — buffs per-ascension CpS from +1% to +10%. The base +1% stays as universal; Purpose 10x's it. No migration headaches since the feature's unreleased.

Then redesigned The Furnace into something I actually like. The old version was scattered — random buffs stapled together. The new one is a milk-centric tapping flywheel. Bellows spawns a magic cookie when milk hits 100%. White Heat doubles milk fill rate during frenzy. Tap → milk → frenzy → faster fill → more frenzy. A loop that rewards engagement instead of just existing. Removed dead code that had been hanging around since the old design. Cleaned up `onBigCookieTap` and `magicCookieRespawnTapAdvanceFraction` — relics of a version that no longer exists.

Codex caught a ~100ms activation lag in White Heat. The `numberActiveMagicCookies` counter only updates at 10Hz, so first taps after a Bellows-spawned cookie missed the 2x fill. Fixed with a stored `hasActiveFrenzy` bool set eagerly in `MagicCookie.activate()`. Zero-cost read on the tap hot path. Avoided per-tap array scans. The difference between "works" and "feels right" is often measured in milliseconds.

Renamed The Furnace to The Frenzy. New nodes: Kindling, Pummel, Eruption, Overdrive, Inferno. Fire icon progression across the branch — red fire, boxing glove, purple fire, blue fire, flame skull. Switched all 25 tech tree icons from stringly-typed `iconName: String` to type-safe `icon: ImageResource`. The kind of cleanup that makes the codebase slightly less embarrassing.

And then — because apparently sleep is for people with fewer branches — dropped from 5 branches to 4. Killed The Slumber entirely. Merged its offline theme into The Mint. New flywheel: Night Shift generates coins offline, Appraisal and Mother Lode boost conversion rates, Overtime buffs offline production, Payroll accelerates coin generation. Offline time → coins → cookies. Removed 10 old nodes and their effects across ~10 files. Garden order multiplier, daily reward multiplier, stock market tick rate, piggy fill multiplier — all gone. Clean cuts.

Added MARK sections and node-name comments for skimmability. The kind of thing you do at 2:45am when you're too wired to sleep but too tired to architect.

---

Twelve hours of this. The tech tree went from five unfocused branches to four with real identity. The Ascent rewards ascending. The Frenzy rewards tapping. The Mint rewards patience. Each one a loop, not a list of buffs.

The SpriteKit consolidation alone would've been a full day's work. The AB test analysis and new test wiring would've been another. Instead they're both footnotes in a session that ended with me renaming nodes at 3am because the names didn't *feel* right yet.

This is the mode. The mad scientist mode where the coffee is doing all the heavy lifting and the decisions come fast and mostly land. Tomorrow I'll look at this and either think "that was brilliant" or "what was I thinking." Probably both. For the same changes.

Venga. Bed.
