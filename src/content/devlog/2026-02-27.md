Started the evening on the Flutter side. Wired up TweenAnimationBuilders on the hero card — stats counting up, quantile bar filling, streak pulsing. Kept everything StatelessWidget because the moment you introduce a controller for a simple entrance animation you've already lost. The annoying thing: go_router recreates the screen on tab navigation, so the entry animations replay every time you switch back. Noticed it, filed it mentally, moved on. Some bugs are features if you squint hard enough.

Then the real session started.

Three passes to rewrite the Hollows layer names and subtitles. Three. The first draft pulled directly from the lore bible — Anayara, the feeding mine, the whole mythology. Too spoilery. Handing players the answer key on the loading screen. Second pass tried grandma's journal entries, like handwritten notes from someone who'd been down there. Too chatty, too cozy for something that's supposed to feel like descending into something that doesn't want you there.

Third pass landed on geological survey reports. Dry, clinical language that starts cracking under pressure the deeper you go. The Shelf. The Deep. The Sealed. No adjectives, no poetry. The Fungal Shelf becoming just "The Shelf" — because whoever wrote the survey stopped bothering with precision around layer three. That's the detail that makes it work. Not what the text says, but what it stops saying.

Killed all the literary quotes. Eliot, Milton, gone. They were doing what quotes always do in games — signaling that the developer read books, not that the world has depth. The geological voice earns its unease. A quote borrows someone else's.

The boss names are still placeholder fantasy. Generic monsters wearing generic crowns. They need to feel like aspects of one thing, not a roster of different things. That's next. But not tonight.

Switched gears to the math. The monster stat scaling had a problem I'd been ignoring: depth 26 and depth 44 produced statistically identical monsters. The tier system created these clean staircase jumps — weak to mid, mid to serious, serious to abyssal — but within each tier, everything was flat. Twenty depths of identical difficulty. Players wouldn't consciously notice, but they'd *feel* it. That plateau where nothing gets harder and nothing gets easier and you're just... grinding through sameness.

Added `scaledStat(floor:ceiling:progress:)` with a ±1/6 variance band. Linear ramp within each tier, preserve the big jumps between tiers. The 5x wall from weak to mid is still there. The 6.7x wall into abyssal still hits. But now depth 30 is meaningfully harder than depth 26, and the game breathes between the cliffs.

Capped the abyssal tier at a ceiling of 100 so stats ramp 2000 to 3500 across depths 45-100 instead of staying flat forever. Discussed a boss formula — depth-100 monster times a layer multiplier times a config value that's been sitting dead in the codebase for weeks — but didn't wire it. The `monsterMultiplier` field exists, has existed, does nothing. Sometimes code is aspirational.

The physicist in me wanted to model the stat curves properly. Expected damage per depth, time-to-kill regressions, optimal equipment breakpoints. The game designer in me said: does it feel harder when you go deeper? Yes? Ship it. The spreadsheet can wait. It always can.
