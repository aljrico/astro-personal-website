Started the evening on the Flutter side. Wired up TweenAnimationBuilder on the hero card stats — smooth 800ms integer tweens, a slower thousand-millisecond crawl on the quantile bar, streak count doing its thing. Kept everything StatelessWidget, no controllers, no State classes. Declarative animation the way it should be. Ripped out `_StatColumn`, replaced it with `_AnimatedStatColumn`, dead code gone, analyzer clean. Felt good.

Then discovered go_router recreates HomeScreen on tab navigation. Which means the entry animations replay every time you switch back. Didn't fix it. Noted it. Moved on.

The Hollows lore was the real session.

I'd been sitting with the layer names for a while and they were wrong. Too literary, too borrowed. Had Eliot quotes, Milton references — the kind of thing that sounds sophisticated in a design doc and falls flat in-game. Nobody's reading T.S. Eliot between combat rounds.

Three passes to find the voice. First attempt was lore-direct: spelled out the mythology, hinted at Anayara, the feeding mine. Too spoilery. Second pass went grandma journals — personal, chatty, first-person accounts from miners. Too warm for what the Hollows are supposed to feel like. Third pass landed: geological survey reports. Clinical language that starts cracking under pressure the deeper you go. The scientist's composure failing. That's the tone.

Renamed everything. The Fungal Shelf became The Shelf. The Altar Depths became The Deep. The Sealed Abyss became The Sealed. Shorter. Heavier. The subtitles do the storytelling now — survey notes that get increasingly unhinged.

Boss names are still generic fantasy monsters. That's next. They should all feel like aspects of one thing, not a bestiary. But not tonight.

Then the scaling problem. Realized that a depth-26 monster and a depth-44 monster were statistically identical. Same HP, same damage. Eighteen floors of nothing changing. That's not difficulty progression, that's a flat line with a costume change.

Built `scaledStat(floor:ceiling:progress:)` with ±1/6 variance. Stats now ramp linearly within each tier while preserving the staircase jumps between tiers — 5x into mid-tier, 5x into serious, 6.7x into abyssal. The walls are still walls. But the hallways between them actually slope upward now.

Capped the abyssal tier at ceiling 100 so stats ramp 2000→3500 over depths 45-100 instead of sitting flat. Discussed the boss formula — depth-100 monster times a layer multiplier times a config value that's currently dead code. Didn't wire it. The formula's right but the plumbing isn't there yet.

Three different kinds of work today. Animations, prose, math. The prose took the most passes. The math was the most satisfying. The animations were the most fun. Not sure what that says about anything.
