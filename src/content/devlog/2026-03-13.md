Two-project day. The kind where you wake up in one codebase and go to bed in another and somewhere in the middle you forget which app you're even building.

Started on the cookie clicker. Islands tech tree needed its skeleton — 25 nodes across 5 branches, the whole data model, persistence, tab nav, placeholder UI. Eight new files. Kept it deliberately dumber than Hell's protocol-heavy approach. Nodes are boolean, not multi-level. Enums with computed properties instead of per-node classes. Sometimes the right architecture is the boring one.

Then the interesting decision: ripped out the passive +10% CpS per island level. Just gone. Islands no longer give production bonuses — the tech tree replaces that entire reward model. Existing players will silently lose their multiplier. I thought about migration paths for about thirty seconds before deciding: nah. It's a beta branch. Move fast.

The wobbly squircles were the fun part. Swapped Circle() for WobblySquircleShape on the tech nodes, deterministic seeds so each node gets its own unique wobble. Replaced the star SF Symbol with the black egg icon. Small stuff, but this is where the flow lives — making things look right. The moment the nodes stopped looking like placeholder circles and started looking like *objects in a world*. That's the hit.

Then the tap freeze. Hostia.

Users tapping the big cookie were getting ~400ms freezes. Unacceptable. Console streaming didn't work for diagnosing it so I went caveman: toast-based timing instrumentation. Tap → measure → toast the milliseconds. Narrowed it down over multiple build cycles: click() → onTap() → addNumber() → playSound(). The culprit was a refactor from weeks ago that replaced SKAction.playSoundFileNamed with AVAudioPlayer(data:). Sounds reasonable on paper. Except AVAudioPlayer decodes MP3 on the main thread. Every. Single. Tap. Four hundred milliseconds of audio decoding blocking the UI thread on a tap handler. Joder.

Tried audio player pooling first. Didn't help. Tried scene isPaused toggling — 384ms wake-up cost, somehow worse. Even consulted a GPT. Dead ends, all of them. The fix was stupid simple: route audio back through SKAction on an always-mounted scene, throttle idle to 1fps. Two hours of detective work for a solution I could describe in one sentence. That's debugging.

Somewhere in the afternoon I pivoted to Flama Coach. The settings bottom sheet, the nested Navigator for in-sheet navigation, the legal copy. Extracted all the inline legal text into markdown assets with platform-conditional blocks — {{ios}} and {{android}} tags resolved by regex at load time. Wrote actual terms and conditions. Subscription cancellation per platform, limitation of liability, the whole thing. The kind of work that feels like homework but matters when Apple reviews it.

Rewrote the method page. "El mètode Flama" — sell the value prop first, science second. Three branded pillars up front, citations after. Brand-first structure. Verified the "milers de preguntes" claim against the database: 3,582 questions across 48 topics. Honest marketing. What a concept.

The App Store review blockers were the real stress. Had a tappable profile tile — "Estudiant" with a chevron — pointing to absolutely nothing. Zero auth behind it. Guaranteed rejection. Killed it entirely. Added subscription management to the premium tile with platform-aware URLs. The kind of cleanup that isn't exciting but saves you a week of back-and-forth with review.

By midnight I was deep in App Store Connect's subscription localization API, generating JWTs with Node.js crypto because the CLI doesn't support subscription CRUD. Created localizations in three languages, then deleted two of them — app's Catalan-only now. Committed the iPad orientation fix, bumped the version, stripped all the debug logging from the subscription flow.

The subscriptions still show MISSING_METADATA. Probably needs a screenshot uploaded through the web UI. Always one more thing.

Fourteen commits across two projects. I keep count. I shouldn't, but I do.

The tech tree unlock button got restyled to match Hollows at the end — slate blue-grey sampled from the egg icon, MinimalButtonStyle, same sizing proportions. Visual consistency. The tech tree finally looks like it *belongs* instead of being a tourist in its own UI. That kind of thing matters more than it should.

Tomorrow I need to actually wire the tech tree nodes to gameplay effects. The skeleton is solid but it's all scaffolding and no building. The fun part — making the numbers go up differently — is still ahead.
