Today was the good kind of day. The kind where the phone sits on the desk, hot reload wired up to it, and every change shows up on real glass two seconds after I decide it. This is my flow state and I know it. Not architecture. Not algorithms. Just: does this *look* right? No. Tweak. Reload. Yes. Commit. Next.

The morning was the toll booth before the highway. Two full review rounds on the duel branch, twenty findings between them, all fixed, backend migrations pushed to prod. Necessary. Boring. The kind of work that feels like flossing. But it bought me the afternoon, and the afternoon was where the game got *better*.

First move: killed the timer. Duel mode had this cosmetic count-up timer and a "Board 2 of 3" chip eating the top of the screen, and the rival — the whole point of the mode — was buried in a card somewhere. Now the rival lives in the header. Small avatar, name, flag, three thin progress bars. You glance up and you see who you're racing. The board gets the vertical space back. Deleting the timer felt great. It was information nobody needed, dressed up as UI.

Then flags. Turns out emoji flags simply don't render on Android, which I'd been quietly ignoring. Shipped all 265 circle-flag SVGs, one shared widget, replaced emoji everywhere — rival banner, league rows, daily leaderboard. A megabyte of flags. Worth it.

The progress bars got their own story. Ported a pill bar from another project, made the duel bars thinner, and the simplify pass caught something I'd fully missed: I was re-targeting a 350ms tween every 180ms tick. Six animation tickers that never, ever settle, all frames, all the time. The kind of bug that doesn't look like a bug — it looks like smooth animation. Fixed it and felt a little sheepish about how confidently I'd written it the first time.

The duel intro was the moment of the day, though. The tagline started as "complete 3 Medium boards" — accurate, dead on arrival. We went back and forth and landed on: *"Three cases. One rival. Fastest detective wins."* Typewriter reveal, clue-panel typography. It types itself out like the game is talking to you. I kept reloading it just to watch it type. That's how I know it's right.

The typewriter even handed me a real bug as a gift: switch locales mid-reveal and the text clipped its own tail — "rápido" became "rápid". The reveal length was computed once at mount and never again. Hot reload artifacts are usually noise, but this one was a genuine bug wearing a costume.

Then the icon fight. Wanted a custom trophy in the corner button. First SVG from the icon pack rendered as a phantom black square — flutter_svg doesn't do CSS classes, and its parse cache survives hot reload, so even the *fix* looked broken until I hot restarted. Joder. Ended up building my own little pipeline: mask the PNG, trim, trace it at 4x into a single clean path. By the second icon — a stats glyph for the daily challenge — the pipeline was routine. Twenty minutes from "I want this icon" to committed. There's a specific pleasure in a hack that graduates into a tool.

Six commits since lunch, roughly one every twenty minutes. The agentic loop at full speed: I decide, the machine types, the phone shows me. No friction anywhere. Days like this are why I do it this way.

The evening was the paperwork after the party. Merged the duel branch into the release branch — duel mode officially rides 1.8.0 now. No more feature-branch limbo. The merge itself was the usual archaeology: conflicting analytics vocabulary, two versions of the same back button, a conversion event that got cherry-picked from both sides and was quietly counting itself twice. Union here, keep the shared widget there, delete the double. Careful, unglamorous work. The kind you only notice if it's done wrong.

And one small scare, entirely self-inflicted. I've been running two sessions in parallel, and at some point I switched the working copy under one of them — new branch, half-finished thermal analytics sitting there uncommitted. For a minute it looked like the afternoon's recede fix had just evaporated. It was in the stash. Everything was in the stash. Pop, resolve, breathe. Nothing lost — but when the machine types this fast across two conversations that both think they own the working directory, I'm the only version control that actually understands what's going on. Worth remembering.

Suite green, 130 of 130, with the in-progress work still applied. Backend side merged and pushed too. Simulator back up, now running the release branch.

The quiet part, briefly: duel mode still doesn't exist for anyone but me. All this polish — the flags, the typewriter, the trophy — is for a mode nobody has played. The rival on that banner isn't real, and neither is the audience yet. But as of tonight it's on the train. 1.8.0 leaves the station with it aboard, and then we find out.

Venga. More tomorrow.
