Today was one of those days. The good kind. Six commits, all visual, all on duel mode, all driven by hot reload on an actual device in my hand. This is where the flow lives for me — not in architecture diagrams, in watching the screen change half a second after I decide it should.

The morning was the tax before the fun: two full rounds of multi-agent review on the duel branch. Twenty findings, all fixed, backend migrations pushed to prod. Necessary. Unmemorable. The moment that was done I switched into visual mode and didn't leave until dinner.

First thing that clicked: the rival card didn't belong in the middle of the screen. It belonged in the top bar. Small avatar, name, flag, three thin progress bars, tucked between the back and hint buttons. The cosmetic timer and the "board X of Y" chip both died to make room, and the board got the space back. The screen instantly felt less cluttered. Some elements you only realize were noise the moment you delete them.

Then flags. Emoji flags don't render on Android — of course they don't — so I shipped all 265 circle-flag SVGs and one widget to rule them all. A megabyte of flags. Worth it.

The progress bars got their own moment. I ported a pill bar from another project, made it thinner, animated. And here's the humbling bit: the simplify pass caught that my animation was re-targeting a 350ms tween every 180ms — six tickers that never settle, burning 60fps forever. It looked fine. It was quietly on fire. Fixed it, felt slightly stupid, moved on.

Best moment of the day: the duel intro tagline. Started with mechanical copy, "complete 3 Medium boards," and iterated until it became *"Three cases. One rival. Fastest detective wins."* Typewriter reveal, clue-panel typography. That's the game's voice. When the copy stops describing the mechanic and starts sounding like the world, something locks in. I kept replaying the reveal animation just to watch it type. And the reveal itself handed me a real bug for free — switching locale mid-screen clipped the tail of the text, "rápido" becoming "rápid." A bug you'd never write a test for. Hot reload found it by accident. That's the whole argument for building this way.

The afternoon closed with icons. First SVG from the icon pack rendered as a phantom black square — flutter_svg doesn't do CSS classes, and its parse cache survives hot reload just to gaslight you. Hostia. So I built my own pipeline: mask the PNG, trim, trace with potrace at 4x, single clean path. Did it once for the trophy. Did it again for the stats icon twenty minutes later. Second time it was routine. There's a specific satisfaction in a hack graduating into a pipeline.

No doubt spiral tonight. Just tired in the good way. The duel screens finally look like the thing I had in my head, and tomorrow there's still a mode to finish. Venga.
