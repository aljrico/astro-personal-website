Started with a bug report that wasn't a bug. The streak flame on the stats banner looked washed-out on the simulator but vibrant on my phone. Classic "works on my machine" except inverted. Spent a minute convinced something was wrong with the rendering pipeline.

Nothing was wrong. The flame dims to 0.22 opacity when you haven't practiced today. Fresh simulator means streak zero, no practice, sad ghost flame. My phone had a two-day streak and a session in the bag, so full glory. The "bug" was the feature working exactly as designed.

So I killed the feature. Why am I dimming a flame to punish someone for not having opened the app yet today? This isn't Duolingo. We decided that on day one — no guilt mechanics. The flame burns at full strength now, always. Ripped out the opacity const, the conditional, the four `withValues(alpha:)` calls. Felt good. Deleting code that exists only to make people feel bad is the best kind of deleting.

Then I got pulled into color. The light-mode banner was this pale cream band with timid orange text while the dark mode had a proper amber flame with white text and a gold inner glow. Two different apps wearing the same name. So I made light mode mirror dark — `#FBBF24` amber background, white text, the real flame. Iterated live on the sim, hot reload, tweak the band alpha, reload. Tried 0.5, too heavy, dropped to 0.1. This is the part I actually love — the screen does the thing in your head, then you nudge it, then it's right. Flow lives here, in the visual. Not in the architecture.

The white-on-amber is borderline WCAG. I know. At 52px and weight 900 it's fine and I'm not going to pretend otherwise.

Afternoon was the restore-purchase bug. Tap "Restaurar compra" a few times fast and you'd get a stack of snackbars pancaked on top of each other. The reentrancy guard missed taps because RevenueCat returns cached results so fast that taps landed in the gap between completions. Added a proper disabled state to the row — 50% opacity, dead InkWell, the works — and made the snackbar clear before showing so only the latest one survives. Couldn't test it live; StoreKit sandbox needs a real purchase flow the simulator won't give me. Analyze clean, hot reload, trust and ship.

Three commits, all to single files, all polish. No new feature. Nobody's going to download this app because the light-mode flame is now the correct shade of amber. But that's the job some days — making the thing not look broken to the one person who notices. Today that person was me on a fresh simulator, briefly fooled by my own code into thinking I'd shipped something dim.

Then mid-afternoon I actually built something. The manual reader had these "Para recordar" sections buried in the prose — the stuff you're supposed to actually memorize — rendered exactly like everything else around them. Invisible. So I pulled them out into accent callout cards: a full-width amber band, a "RECUERDA!" eyebrow, arrow bullets, heavier body weight inside. The kind of thing that makes you go *oh, this part matters* without reading a word.

Getting there meant fighting flutter_markdown for an hour. I wanted custom block tags, but they're not in the package's block list, so the parser just ignores them. Tried a custom element builder. Dead end. Ended up splitting the markdown into segments — prose, callout, prose, callout — and interleaving real Markdown widgets with my own card widget in a ListView. Less elegant. Works. Code quality is overrated and I've made my peace with that years ago.

The gotcha that ate twenty minutes: MarkdownBody only re-parses when the data or stylesheet changes, *not* when the bullet builder changes. So I'm tweaking bullet icons, hot reloading, seeing nothing, tweaking again, seeing nothing, slowly going insane. Hot *restart* and there they are. Of course. The framework was right and I was the idiot, as usual.

And then — venga — I shipped it. Bumped the build, built the IPA, uploaded, watched it go VALID. But here's the thing I'd half-forgotten: the App Store still showed 1.0.1 as the live version. Everything since had been TestFlight-only. So I cut a real App Store version, copied over last release's localizations — the description, the keywords, the promo text — attached the build, and pushed it to review. Waiting for review now. Out of my hands.

That's the quiet part. The build's been processing, the metadata's copied, the submit button's pressed, and now there's just... waiting. Some reviewer somewhere, eventually, will tap through my amber flame and my callout cards and decide. And if it passes, it goes live, and real people studying for a real exam will see it. Or won't. The app's been out at 1.0.1 this whole time and I genuinely don't know if anyone's using it. Pushing 1.0.3 to the store feels less like a launch and more like leaving a note for someone who may or may not read it.

But the callout cards look good. They look *right*. I keep scrolling the manual just to see them sit there in their little amber bands, telling you what matters. That part I'm sure about.
