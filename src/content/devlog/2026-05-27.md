Started the day chasing a ghost.

Bug report: the streak flame on the stats banner looked washed-out on the simulator, vibrant on my phone. Same code, two different worlds. The physicist brain immediately went to rendering — color spaces, sRGB vs display-P3, some simulator gamma quirk. I was already three tabs deep into how iOS handles wide color.

Then I actually read my own code. The flame dims to 0.22 opacity when you haven't practiced today. Fresh simulator: streak 0, no practice, washed out. My phone: streak 2, practiced, full strength. Not a rendering bug. A feature working exactly as designed, just designed badly. The flame was telling the truth and I didn't like the truth.

So I killed the dimming. A flame at 22% opacity isn't a flame, it's an apology. Now it burns full strength regardless. Deleted the opacity const, the conditional, the four `withValues(alpha:)` calls. Felt good. Less code, more fire.

Then I got pulled into the light mode banner, which I'd apparently never finished. Dark mode had this gorgeous amber flame treatment and light mode had a sad pale-cream band with orange text like a receipt. Couldn't leave it. Hot reload on the sim, tweak the hex, hit 'r', look, tweak again. Amber background, white text, orange flame with a gold inner glow. This is the part I actually love — the loop where you change one value and the screen changes back at you instantly. Tried the band at 0.5 alpha, too loud, dropped it to 0.1 so the banner and the calendar band stay related but don't shout. The white-on-amber contrast is borderline WCAG but it's 52px and bold-900 so it reads fine. Looks right. That's the bar.

Afternoon was less fun. Restore-purchase button stacking snackbars — tap it five times, get five banners queuing up like a polite British line. The reentrancy guard was there but RevenueCat returns so fast from cache that taps slip through between completions. Added a proper `enabled` flag to the row — half opacity, kills the InkWell, flips the Semantics — and made the snackbar clear before showing so only the latest one survives. Ran code-review on it, one low-sev note about clearing the app-global queue, but there are no other snackbar sources in that sheet so the blast radius is nothing.

Couldn't actually test the restore live. Needs a StoreKit sandbox purchase and the simulator won't play along. So it's `flutter analyze` clean and a hope. I hate shipping on a hope, but some bugs you can't reproduce on your machine no matter how badly you want to.

Three fixes. None of them hard. All of them the kind of thing that makes the app feel less like a prototype and more like a thing someone made on purpose. No grand feature today. Just sanding down splinters.

The flame burns full now. That's something.
