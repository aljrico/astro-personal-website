```markdown
Started with a bug report that wasn't a bug. The streak flame on the stats banner looked washed-out on the simulator but vibrant on my phone. Classic "works on my machine" except inverted. Spent a minute convinced something was wrong with the rendering pipeline.

Nothing was wrong. The flame dims to 0.22 opacity when you haven't practiced today. Fresh simulator means streak zero, no practice, sad ghost flame. My phone had a two-day streak and a session in the bag, so full glory. The "bug" was the feature working exactly as designed.

So I killed the feature. Why am I dimming a flame to punish someone for not having opened the app yet today? This isn't Duolingo. We decided that on day one — no guilt mechanics. The flame burns at full strength now, always. Ripped out the opacity const, the conditional, the four `withValues(alpha:)` calls. Felt good. Deleting code that exists only to make people feel bad is the best kind of deleting.

Then I got pulled into color. The light-mode banner was this pale cream band with timid orange text while the dark mode had a proper amber flame with white text and a gold inner glow. Two different apps wearing the same name. So I made light mode mirror dark — `#FBBF24` amber background, white text, the real flame. Iterated live on the sim, hot reload, tweak the band alpha, reload. Tried 0.5, too heavy, dropped to 0.1. This is the part I actually love — the screen does the thing in your head, then you nudge it, then it's right. Flow lives here, in the visual. Not in the architecture.

The white-on-amber is borderline WCAG. I know. At 52px and weight 900 it's fine and I'm not going to pretend otherwise.

Afternoon was the restore-purchase bug. Tap "Restaurar compra" a few times fast and you'd get a stack of snackbars pancaked on top of each other. The reentrancy guard missed taps because RevenueCat returns cached results so fast that taps landed in the gap between completions. Added a proper disabled state to the row — 50% opacity, dead InkWell, the works — and made the snackbar clear before showing so only the latest one survives. Couldn't test it live; StoreKit sandbox needs a real purchase flow the simulator won't give me. Analyze clean, hot reload, trust and ship.

Then the late session, and this one actually had a feature in it. The manual reader was rendering everything as one flat slab of Markdown — including the "Para recordar" sections, which are the whole point. Those are the things you're supposed to *remember*, and they were drowning in the same grey prose as everything else. So I pulled them out into accent callout cards: a full-width band at 0.25 alpha, a "RECUERDA!" eyebrow, FontAwesome arrow-right bullets, bolder body inside. The kind of thing that makes the eye stop where it should stop.

Getting there meant splitting the document into segments and interleaving prose and callouts in a ListView instead of feeding one blob to `MarkdownBody`. I went down the custom-block-syntax road first — register a tag, write a `MarkdownElementBuilder` — and bounced off it. The custom tag isn't in flutter_markdown's block list, so it'd never fire. Segment-splitting was the dumber, faster path and it worked. Code quality is overrated; this was the right kind of lazy.

The gotcha that ate ten minutes: `MarkdownBody` in 0.7.7 only re-parses when `data` or `styleSheet` changes, *not* when you swap the `bulletBuilder`. So I'm tweaking bullet icons, hot reloading, nothing moves. Convinced for a moment I'd broken something. Nope — hot *restart* and there it is. Hostia, every framework has one of these.

And then — this is the part that surprised me — I shipped it. Not to TestFlight and forget. Actually shipped. Bumped to +60, built the IPA, uploaded, it processed clean. Then I noticed the App Store still had 1.0.1 live — every version since had been TestFlight-only, quietly piling up where nobody could see them. So I created the 1.0.3 App Store version, copied the old localizations over, attached the build, and submitted it for review. WAITING_FOR_REVIEW. A real thing, going to real people, for the first time in a while.

Three commits in the morning that nobody would ever notice, and one in the evening that might. Funny how the day inverted itself. I came in fixing a flame that was never broken and left having pushed the thing that's been sitting in the TestFlight drawer for weeks. The callout cards won't make anyone download the app either. But they make the manual look like someone cared, and tonight someone did.
```
