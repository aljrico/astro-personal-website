Copy day. No code. Just words.

Sat down to punch up Verdoku's App Store description. Had a fresh draft I was pretty pleased with. Then I put it side by side with what's actually live and, well. The live version was better. Almost everywhere. That's a very specific flavor of humbling: losing an argument with your past self. He was sharper. He always is.

But the draft wasn't a total loss. Three ideas survived the autopsy, which feels appropriate for this game. The big one is the new opener: "Murder Sudoku is like Sudoku, but every puzzle is a crime scene." That's the line. The whole game in one sentence, sitting above the fold where it actually gets read. Kept the "moment of the murder" framing too. And finally added a leaderboards bullet, because I shipped competitive leaderboards a while back and the store copy just never heard about it. The description was selling a game from months ago. So the final version is a merge: the live copy's skeleton, the draft's three good organs. Best of both, sewn together. Very on brand.

Then the em-dash purge. I'm convinced em-dashes are an AI-tell at this point, and this copy is machine-assisted, so out they went. Every single one, swapped for commas and colons like a normal person writes. There's something funny about laundering the machine out of your own prose. Yes, I see the irony. This diary included.

Spanish and Catalan got the same pass, keeping the casual register: no opening ¡ or ¿, just "Atascado?" like an actual person texting you. Nobody at Apple will ever notice. But it's the difference between copy that sounds like a human and copy that sounds like a localization vendor, and I notice.

Small discovery along the way: the British English locale doesn't even carry a description, just promo text and what's new. The UK gets spared the whole rewrite by sheer bureaucratic accident. Lucky them.

The restrained part of me left the Android listing alone. It's its own ASO animal, chasing its own keywords, leaning on the Daily Challenge, and when I actually went to check, it already covers the leaderboards. Past me, sharper again. Ten-am me would've rewritten it just for the momentum. Small victory for knowing when to stop.

Committed the whole thing at 11:21. One commit, one branch, three .strings files, zero lines of code. It all rides the next production release, so today's work is invisible until then. Words in a queue, waiting for a train.
