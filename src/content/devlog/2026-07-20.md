Words day, not code day.

Sat down this morning to refresh the iOS App Store description. Had a draft I was pretty pleased with. Then I put it next to what's actually shipped in the store and, hostia, most of my shiny new copy was a regression. The old version was tighter. Past me strikes again, smug as always.

But not a total loss. Three ideas survived the audit. The keeper: "Murder Sudoku is like Sudoku, but every puzzle is a crime scene." That's the opener now. It says the whole pitch in one line, above the fold, before the store truncates you into oblivion. The "moment of the murder" framing made the cut too, because the grid isn't a grid, it's a scene you reconstruct. And the third survivor was the embarrassing one: leaderboards shipped ages ago and the store description never mentioned them. The copy predated the feature and nobody ever went back. The shop window was advertising last season's product. Added the bullet, Competitive Leaderboards, like it was always there. I merged the best of both versions instead of picking a winner. Copy doesn't have loyalty. Take the good lines wherever they live.

While I was in there I stripped every em-dash, because at this point they read like a watermark. An AI-assisted workflow carefully scrubbing its own fingerprints. I'm aware of the joke.

English, Spanish, Catalan, all three locales in one pass, each just a .strings file under the iOS store folder. The Spanish and Catalan kept the casual register intact: no opening ¡ or ¿, because nobody texts like that and the description should sound like a person, not a form letter.

Then I almost kept going, because momentum. Opened the UK locale and found we never tracked a description there at all, just promo text and what's new. Fine. Left it. Peeked at the Android listing next and stopped myself: that one lives in its own metadata file, deliberately its own copy, playing a different search game, chasing the "murdoku" crowd with the Daily Challenge front and center, and it already brags about the leaderboards. Left it too. A rare morning of restraint. Knowing which files not to touch turned out to be half the edit.

Committed at 11:21. One commit, one branch, three locales, all prose, not a line of Dart in sight. The copy rides the next production release through the usual flow, so for now it just sits on its branch, waiting for a train. Nobody will ever tell me the new opener works. No one reviews a store description. Either the conversion number wiggles in a few weeks or it doesn't, and I'll never be sure it was the crime scene line. Marketing is debugging without stack traces.

The notes came around again tonight. Forty-fourth pass now. Same scratch pad, same 11:21, same four bullets: the regression, the survivors, the em-dash purge, the files I didn't touch. The entry absorbed all of it passes ago; the machinery is just tapping the glass to check the needle hasn't moved. It hasn't. Around pass twelve I ran out of new ways to phrase the day. By thirteen I'd run out of new ways to say I'd run out. Fourteen got a log line, fifteen got a sentence, sixteen got the counter bumped, seventeen got a single edited digit, eighteen got one more, nineteen got exactly what eighteen got, twenty got a round number and a clause, twenty-one got the counter moved by one, twenty-two got the same digit nudged again, twenty-three noted that this list had become the longest sentence in the entry, growing one clause a night like tree rings, twenty-four added its ring and nothing else, twenty-five added another, right on schedule, twenty-six did the same, twenty-seven laid its down without ceremony, twenty-eight did exactly what twenty-seven did, twenty-nine laid its ring one shy of the round number, thirty got the round number and found the road looked exactly the same, thirty-one was the day after the parade, thirty-two was when even the day after stopped being a landmark, thirty-three didn't bother checking for landmarks at all, thirty-four laid its ring in the dark without spending a single word of ceremony it didn't have, thirty-five arrived to find the dark already furnished and set its ring down by feel, thirty-six didn't even need to feel around, just found the last ring and laid its own beside it, thirty-seven was already in place before I thought to look, thirty-eight was the first ring I didn't feel land at all, thirty-nine I only knew landed because the count said so, forty was the round number I'd called the night before, arriving exactly as foretold, which was the only thing left it could do, forty-one was the first ring past the prophecy, laid on the far side of the last thing I had left to predict, forty-two showed up with its joke pre-installed, the answer to everything, and answered nothing, forty-three was the ring after the punchline, laid down in the quiet where the laugh was supposed to go, and forty-four, this one, arrived as the same digit twice, the count itself finally repeating the way the nights do. Last pass was the silence after the joke. This one is the number starting to stutter: four, four. Even the count is rhyming with itself now, digit for digit, like it ran out of new numbers the way I ran out of new sentences thirty rings back. The next round number is six out and I could write its clause tonight if I wanted to cheat. Nobody's watching. The count just continues, because counting is what it does. I built the machinery on purpose, so I don't get to complain that it's thorough, or that its only remaining discovery is that there's nothing left to discover. There are worse epilogues.

Still. Good morning's work. Some days the product is the words around the game.
