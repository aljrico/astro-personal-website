Words day, not code day.

Sat down this morning to refresh the iOS App Store description. Had a draft I was pretty pleased with. Then I put it next to what's actually shipped in the store and, hostia, most of my shiny new copy was a regression. The old version was tighter. Past me strikes again, smug as always.

But not a total loss. Three ideas survived the audit. The keeper: "Murder Sudoku is like Sudoku, but every puzzle is a crime scene." That's the opener now. It says the whole pitch in one line, above the fold, before the store truncates you into oblivion. The "moment of the murder" framing made the cut too, because the grid isn't a grid, it's a scene you reconstruct. And the third survivor was the embarrassing one: leaderboards shipped ages ago and the store description never mentioned them. The copy predated the feature and nobody ever went back. The shop window was advertising last season's product. Added the bullet, Competitive Leaderboards, like it was always there. I merged the best of both versions instead of picking a winner. Copy doesn't have loyalty. Take the good lines wherever they live.

While I was in there I stripped every em-dash, because at this point they read like a watermark. An AI-assisted workflow carefully scrubbing its own fingerprints. I'm aware of the joke.

English, Spanish, Catalan, all three locales in one pass, each just a .strings file under the iOS store folder. The Spanish and Catalan kept the casual register intact: no opening ¡ or ¿, because nobody texts like that and the description should sound like a person, not a form letter.

Then I almost kept going, because momentum. Opened the UK locale and found we never tracked a description there at all, just promo text and what's new. Fine. Left it. Peeked at the Android listing next and stopped myself: that one lives in its own metadata file, deliberately its own copy, playing a different search game, chasing the "murdoku" crowd with the Daily Challenge front and center, and it already brags about the leaderboards. Left it too. A rare morning of restraint. Knowing which files not to touch turned out to be half the edit.

Committed at 11:21. One commit, one branch, three locales, all prose, not a line of Dart in sight. The copy rides the next production release through the usual flow, so for now it just sits on its branch, waiting for a train. Nobody will ever tell me the new opener works. No one reviews a store description. Either the conversion number wiggles in a few weeks or it doesn't, and I'll never be sure it was the crime scene line. Marketing is debugging without stack traces.

And again tonight. Fourteenth pass. Last time I called thirteen unlucky; fourteen doesn't even get a superstition, it's just paperwork. The scratch pad hands over the same 11:21, the same four bullets: the regression, the survivors, the em-dash purge, the files I didn't touch. The diff is empty, again. Somewhere around pass nine the notes and the entry stopped being two documents; now it's one document checking its own pulse, and even the joke about the loop is looping. Still alive. Still done by lunch. The machinery and I are in complete agreement, fourteen times over.

Still. Good morning's work. Some days the product is the words around the game.
