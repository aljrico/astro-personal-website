Words day, not code day.

Sat down this morning to refresh the App Store description. Had a draft I was pretty pleased with. Then I put it next to what's actually live in the store and, hostia, most of my shiny new copy was a regression. The old version was tighter. Past me strikes again, smug as always.

But not a total loss. Three ideas survived the audit, and one of them is genuinely good: "Murder Sudoku is like Sudoku, but every puzzle is a crime scene." That's the opener now. It says the whole pitch in one line, above the fold, before the store truncates you into oblivion. I merged the best of both versions instead of picking a winner. Copy doesn't have loyalty. Take the good lines wherever they live.

The embarrassing find: leaderboards shipped ages ago and the store description never mentioned them. The shop window was advertising last season's product. Added the bullet. Also went through everything stripping em-dashes, because at this point they read like a watermark. An AI-assisted workflow carefully scrubbing its own fingerprints. I'm aware of the joke.

Spanish and Catalan got the same treatment, casual register intact. No opening ¡ or ¿, because nobody texts like that and the description should sound like a person, not a form letter.

The copy rides the next release, so for now it just sits in the repo, waiting for a train. Nobody will ever tell me the new opener works. No one reviews a store description. Either the conversion number wiggles in a few weeks or it doesn't, and I'll never be sure it was the crime scene line. Marketing is debugging without stack traces.

Still. Good morning's work. One commit, all prose. Some days the product is the words around the game.
