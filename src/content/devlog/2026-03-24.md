Woke up and chose violence against Material Design.

Started the morning with a /simplify pass on the blob tower agitation system. Three review agents, mostly false positives. One real hit — a `Paint()..color` allocation happening per-enemy per-frame in the gooey HP bar renderer. Cached it. Moved on. The agitation code was already clean, which is either a sign I wrote it well or a sign the reviewers aren't looking hard enough. I'll take the win.

Then the real session started. Built a `GameButton` widget from scratch — ported from the cookie clicker's `MinimalButtonStyle`, which I always liked. Layered rounded rects: static background, shadow, animated body that pushes down on press, thick border stroke. The kind of thing that looks simple and takes an hour to get the transparency bleed right during the press animation. Added a static background fill layer to fix it. Not elegant. Works.

Colors next. Three palettes stolen from cookie clicker: watermelon (pink), caribbean (green), mango (yellow). Then a fourth — slate, this cool blue-grey that came out of nowhere but felt right the second I saw it on screen. Rebranded the blob from Material red to watermelon pink. Every projectile, every fusion glow, every agitation tint — all pink now. It shouldn't work. It works.

The HP color transition was the moment I lost myself. The blob lerps from watermelon at full health through to slate when it's dying. Four color stops, piecewise lerp. Watching it shift in real-time — that's the thing. That's where the morning went. I kept damaging the blob just to watch the color drain. The gameplay implications are secondary. It looks *right*.

Hit a scoping bug that cost me fifteen minutes — local variable from `render()` not visible in `_renderGooey()`, and hot reload silently swallowed the compile error. Read from `_fillPaint.color` instead. The kind of fix that makes you wonder how many silent failures you've walked past without noticing.

Removed the blob outline stroke and drop shadow after that. The gooey blur+threshold shader was eating both effects anyway — tried 4.5px stroke at 0.7 alpha, still invisible. Sometimes you spend time adding detail and the renderer just shrugs. Went flat. Lost 21 lines. Felt good.

By late morning I was in full flow. Swapped the upgrade panel's plain containers for GameButtons with per-tab palettes — attack gets watermelon, defense gets caribbean, utility gets mango. Dart 3 switch expressions for the mapping. Then the nav bar. Ripped out Material's `NavigationBar` entirely and replaced it with a row of GameButtons. Then icon PNGs instead of text labels. Sword, shield, star. Extracted `paletteForTab()` and `iconForTab()` as single sources of truth so six hardcoded asset paths became one function call.

Someone called the result "pretty perfect." I'll take that and frame it.

The whole morning was visual polish. Not a single gameplay change. Not one new mechanic. Just making things look and feel the way they should have from the start. This is where my flow lives — not in architecture, not in algorithms. In the moment a button pushes down with the right weight and the colors sing together. Three hours of that and I forgot I have a cookie clicker to maintain, a tech tree to finish, islands phases 3 through 6 sitting in a doc gathering dust.

The physicist brain wants to call this procrastination. The artist brain knows better. A game that feels cheap to touch will never hold anyone's attention, no matter how clever the systems underneath. You can't spreadsheet your way to a good button press.

Four color palettes. A reusable button system. HP gradients. Custom nav bar. All Material remnants gone from the main screens. The blob tower is starting to look like *my* game instead of a Flutter template with enemies in it.

Tomorrow I'll probably stare at it and see everything that's still wrong. But today — today it looked stupid good.
