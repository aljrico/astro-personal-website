Built the whole streak feature today. Model, service, provider, widget, database migration — the full vertical slice, zero to TestFlight in one session. The kind of afternoon where you sit down and the code just *goes*.

The idea is simple: show a practice streak card on the home screen. Seven-day rolling calendar, today in the center, three days back, three days forward. Duolingo does this and it works because it's low-key guilt. You see the empty circles and you think, one more session won't hurt. Behavioral design. Slightly manipulative. Effective.

The 2AM grace period was a fun one to think about. If you practice at 11pm, you shouldn't lose your streak because you forgot to close the app before midnight. So the "day" doesn't roll over until 2AM. Small thing. The kind of detail nobody notices unless you get it wrong.

Minimum streak is 1, never 0. Because showing someone a big fat zero on day one is hostile UX. You opened the app. You're here. That counts for something.

Then the polish spiral started. You know the one.

Switched the day labels to Barlow Semi Condensed at 12px because the default was too wide and the circles were crowding each other. Tried lowercase Catalan abbreviations — dl, dt, dc — because IEC says so. But capitalized reads better in the actual UI. Sorry, Institut d'Estudis Catalans. Readability wins.

Then the flame icon. Tried the Material filled version, `Icons.local_fire_department`. Looked like clip art from a 2014 PowerPoint. The Lucide outline was already perfect and I should have trusted my first instinct. Twenty minutes to confirm what I already knew. Classic.

The inactive day circles went from hollow outlines to a subtle white fill. Tiny change. The kind of thing that takes thirty seconds to implement and makes you stare at the screen for five minutes afterwards because it just *looks right* and you can't explain why.

Shipped it. Pushed to main, kicked off the TestFlight build. There's a bug — the day labels are static instead of rolling with actual weekdays, so they're wrong for anything that isn't today's specific alignment. Noticed it right at the end. Left it for next session. Ship the thing, fix the labels tomorrow.

The streak card sits on the home screen now with a dark gradient and a senyera stripe across the top. It looks like it belongs there. That feeling — when a new component slots into the existing design and doesn't look bolted on — that's the good stuff. That's why the polish spiral exists. You chase that moment where it clicks.

Tomorrow: fix the weekday labels. Should be fifteen minutes. Will probably be an hour because I'll find something else to tweak.
