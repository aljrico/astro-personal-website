Built the entire streak system in one session. Model, service, provider, widget, database migration, the works. That sentence makes it sound clean. It wasn't. But the pieces fell into place faster than expected and that's the kind of afternoon that reminds you why you do this.

The streak card sits on the home screen now — dark gradient, senyera stripe across the top, seven-day rolling calendar with little Catalan day labels. Dl, Dt, Dc, Dj, Dv, Ds, Dg. There's a 2AM grace period baked into the service because nobody should lose a streak because they were coding until 1:47am. Ask me how I know.

The fun part was the constraint: minimum streak is always 1, never 0. You opened the app. You showed up. That counts. I like that philosophy more than I expected to.

Then the polish spiral started.

Centered today in the week row — three days back, today, three days forward. Duolingo does this and it works because your eye lands on the present. Filled the inactive day circles with a subtle white tint instead of leaving them hollow. Switched to BarlowSemiCondensed at 12px because the day labels were breathing too much.

Tried IEC lowercase labels. dl, dt, dc. Technically more correct. Looked like a whisper. Capitalized them back. Dl, Dt, Dc. Better.

Then I tried swapping the Lucide flame outline for Material's filled `local_fire_department` icon. Hostia, no. It looked like a safety warning on a gas appliance. Reverted in under a minute. Some experiments you run just to confirm your instincts were right.

Found a bug on the way out — the day labels are static, not rolling with actual weekdays. Thursday says Thursday regardless of what day the column actually represents. It's the kind of thing nobody would notice for a week and then it would be all they see. Noted for next time.

Pushed to main. TestFlight build spinning up. The streak card looks good. Not "I need to show everyone" good — more like "this belongs here" good. Quieter satisfaction. The kind where you close the laptop and don't immediately reopen it.

Some features you build because they're on the roadmap. The streak was one of those. But somewhere between the senyera stripe and the 2AM grace period it stopped being a checkbox and started being mine. That's the difference between implementing a feature and designing one. You can't always tell which you're doing until you're done.
