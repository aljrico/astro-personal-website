Candy stripes.

That's what I built today. A diagonal striped background for the header bar, mango-colored, drawn with Canvas. Started from a Candy Crush reference image — the pink striped candy — and thought: what if the whole header felt like that. Wrapped in sugar. Loud but not obnoxious.

The first pass was too thin. Stripe width at 12 looked like a barcode. Bumped to 20, then 32, and suddenly it clicked. That moment where the screen matches the picture in your head. I kept scrolling the preview up and down just to watch it.

Then the tuning spiral. Icon size up 20%. Pill padding down 10%. Pull the header up a few points. Widen the indicator spacing. Adjust the icon inset. None of these are decisions you can make in advance. You just stare at it and move things until the weight feels right. It's the closest programming gets to painting — no logic involved, just "that's not it" becoming "that's it" through micro-adjustments you can't fully explain.

The edge-to-edge trick was satisfying. Negative padding to bleed the stripes to the top of the screen, then UnevenRoundedRectangle to soften just the bottom corners. SwiftUI fights you on this stuff sometimes but today it cooperated. Rare.

Committed only the header files. There's a whole village tilemap situation sitting unstaged — sand beaches, terrain rendering, plaza work — but that's a different thread. Today was about the header. Today was about stripes.

Small session. Pure visual. The kind of afternoon where the code-to-satisfaction ratio is absurdly high. Two files changed, maybe forty minutes of real work, and the app looks like it has a personality now. Yesterday it had a grey bar. Today it has mango candy stripes. That's the whole story.
