Sometimes the best sessions are the ones where you make something stupid and fun and completely unnecessary.

The header bar needed a background. Not functionally — functionally it was fine. But it looked flat. Dead. Like a spreadsheet pretending to be a game. So I pulled up a candy-crush reference image, the pink striped candy, and thought: what if the header had that energy? Diagonal stripes. Mango-colored. Dumb and joyful.

Canvas in SwiftUI is one of those APIs that feels like drawing. You tell it where the lines go and it draws them. No layout engine fighting you. No autoresizing masks from hell. Just geometry. Built the stripe pattern, got it rendering, and then the real work started: making it *feel* right.

Stripe width 12 — too thin, looked like a barcode. Bumped to 20 — getting there. Landed on 32. Then the icon was too small relative to the new boldness, so +20%. Then the pill padding felt bloated, so -10% on that and the corner radius. Pulled the whole header up a few points. Widened the indicator spacing. Adjusted the icon inset.

This is the part I can't explain to people who don't build visual things. Each tweak is a thirty-second cycle: change a number, hot reload, squint, decide. It looks like nothing from the outside. From the inside it's the closest thing to flow I know. The screen slowly becoming the thing you saw in your head.

The negative padding trick to bleed edge-to-edge past the safe area while keeping rounded bottom corners — that one took a minute. UnevenRoundedRectangle. SwiftUI keeps adding these little gifts that solve problems I used to hack around with geometry readers and clip shapes. Progress.

Committed just the header files. The village map has a bunch of sand-beach-terrain work sitting unstaged from earlier and I didn't want to mix the two stories. Clean commits are one of the few code-quality things I actually care about. Not because of craftsmanship — because future me will grep the history at 2am and "feat: add candy-stripe mango background to header bar" tells him exactly what happened. "misc updates" tells him nothing.

Short session. One visual feature. No architecture, no state management, no data flow. Just stripes on a screen. And I keep opening the simulator to look at them.

That's the whole entry. Some days you refactor the world. Some days you draw candy stripes and grin about it.
