Four in the morning drawing tentacles. If you'd told 2020-me this is where the physics degree leads, I'd have believed you. That's the worrying part.

The Deep branch needed icons — five of them, a visual progression from a single tentacle to a full eldritch nightmare. One tentacle, two, three, a cluster, then the final form. The idea was clean: take the existing tentacle SVG, duplicate paths, compose them. Should've been an hour.

It wasn't an hour.

First three came together fast. Single tentacle extracted from the original. Doubles mirrored and offset. Triples arranged in a fan. That was the fun part — the composing, the arranging, the "yeah that looks right" moment. Flow state. The screen doing what my head pictured.

Then the cluster and the eldritch. Drew new paths by hand, curving them, overlapping. Rendered them and — thin. Wispy little lines like spider legs. No presence. No weight. The kind of thing that looks fine zoomed in on the SVG editor and completely disappears at icon size.

The fix was obvious once I saw it: SVG paths drawn as single strokes have no volume. You need closed shapes — left edge, tip, right edge, filled. An actual form, not a line pretending to be one. Redrew them thick, meaty, proper tentacle shapes. The eldritch version got a darker palette — deep greens, almost black, with bright glow hints bleeding through. It looks like something that shouldn't be on a phone screen. I love it.

Also restyled the tech tree detail sheet while I was in there. Duotone header, proper typography, rich descriptions with AttributedStrings, locked nodes showing just a padlock instead of the cluttered state they had before. Small changes but the kind that make the whole thing feel *designed* instead of assembled.

This is always the trap with visual work. I could keep polishing forever. Every icon leads to "well the sheet should match" which leads to "the fonts are wrong" which leads to sunrise. The tentacles look stupid good though. Five of them in a row, building from minimal to cosmic horror. That progression tells a story before you read a word.

Venga. Bed.
