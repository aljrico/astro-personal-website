Two-project day. Ping-ponging between the idle game and FlamaCoach like someone who can't decide which child needs attention. Both needed it. Both got it. Neither is done.

Started with the idle game's ascension formulas. Eighteen of them, each doing its own thing — some linear, some sqrt, some uncapped exponents that scaled into absurdity. The kind of organic mess that happens when you add features over months without a unifying principle. Today I gave them one: `^0.8` diminishing returns across the board. Production boosts, cost modifiers, all of them. Chose 0.8 over sqrt because at level 100, sqrt gives you an effective 10 — a 90% nerf that would make existing players feel robbed. 0.8 gives you ~40, a 60% nerf. Still meaningful, still fair. The physicist brain ran the numbers. The game designer brain said "don't punish people for playing a lot." They agreed for once.

Then the "Necessary Sacrifices" revelation. Cost modifier formula was `0.90^(1.10 * level * log(grandmas))` and at high levels it drove Temple cost to zero. Literally zero. One line fix: wrap the level term in `sqrt()`. Same pattern as the 2^n fix from before. I keep finding the same bug wearing different hats.

The code review detour was funny. Tried Codex CLI, found out o3 isn't supported on ChatGPT subscriptions, fell back to o4-mini. It caught real stuff though — the ProductionMultiplierManager was replacing boosts instead of stacking them. Boosts now multiply together instead of overwriting. Also caught a CADisplayLink running uncapped at 120Hz on ProMotion devices. I'd been burning battery for frames nobody could see.

Then FlamaCoach. The paywall.

Building a paywall is a weird thing. You're making the gate between "free user" and "paying customer" and every pixel of it is a sales pitch. I went RevenueCat SDK v8, built a SubscriptionProvider with SharedPrefs caching, added a UsageService with daily limits — 10 free drills per day, enough to hook but not enough to satisfy. The database migrated from v3 to v4 cleanly. Twelve files, 763 insertions. The biggest single commit in FlamaCoach's history, probably.

Kept the paywall screen custom instead of using RevenueCat's built-in PaywallView. Brand consistency matters more than development speed when the screen's entire job is convincing someone to give you money.

The RevenueCat dashboard wiring had a gotcha that cost me twenty minutes: I'd wired the products to the Test Store app instead of the real App Store app, AND had the wrong API key — `test_` prefix instead of `appl_`. The kind of mistake that makes you feel stupid until you remember every SDK integration has at least one of these. Switched keys, offerings loaded, the sandbox Apple sign-in dialog appeared on purchase tap. Progress.

---

The rest of the afternoon was UI polish and I was in the zone.

The paywall went through three visual identities in two hours. Started with an amber crown badge. Swapped to FontAwesome solid for a filled look. Then scrapped the crown entirely for a custom shield SVG with a white flame overlay. Then added an outline layer — two stacked SVGs, the back one using `paint-order: stroke fill` to push the stroke outward. Tested outline widths from 3px to 8px, settled on 6px. Matched the outline color to the CTA button shadow. Added a vertical stripe texture overlay as a third ShaderMask layer — alternating transparent and 12% white at 4px pitch.

Five layers to render one badge. It looks stupid good.

Tested every combination. Solid navy, navy-to-red gradient, navy-to-amber, stripes-only. Amber flame, red flame, pale blue flame, white flame. White won every time. Sometimes the obvious answer is the right one and you just need to prove it to yourself by trying everything else first.

The streak card got the same treatment. Replaced checkmarks with flame icons in the daily dots, inverted to white circles with amber flames. Hit the classic SVG-in-Container sizing bug — needed `alignment: Alignment.center` for the flame to respect its size parameter instead of expanding to fill. One of those trivially stupid things that breaks flow state harder than any real problem would.

The Senyera stripe went from amber to pale blue (#CDD9EB). Tried navy — too dark. Tried the theme border color — slightly off. Added `accentLight` to AppColors because magic hex values in widget code are a code smell I can actually smell. Same value light and dark since the stripe always sits on a dark gradient.

Price anchoring on the paywall: multi-month plans now show a crossed-out "monthly × months" total next to the actual price. Computed dynamically from RevenueCat's prices so it auto-adapts. Default selection switched from monthly to annual — standard anchoring for highest LTV. The spreadsheet brain is useful when you're building the thing that pays the bills.

The last thing I fixed today was the last thing on the overflow audit. `getClickerAscensionExtraProductivity()` was doing all its math in Double space — BigNumber clamped to Double, multiplied by upgrade count, overflow. Changed return type to BigNumber, kept multiplication in BigNumber space, slapped `^0.8` on the clicker ascension level for consistency. That closes the full audit. Every ascension formula now flows through BigNumber with diminishing returns.
