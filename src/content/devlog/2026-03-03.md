The day started with exponents.

Standardized all eighteen ascension upgrade formulas in the game to use ^0.8 diminishing returns. The physicist brain ran the numbers: at level 100, ^0.8 gives you an effective ~40 — a 60% nerf. Square root would give you 10, which is a 90% nerf. Too brutal for players who've been grinding. The gentler curve respects their time while still capping the runaway scaling that was breaking endgame. Eight production boost formulas. Ten cost modifier formulas. All now following the same pattern, replacing a zoo of linear, sqrt, and uncapped exponents. There's something deeply satisfying about imposing order on chaos.

Then I switched to FlamaCoach and spent the rest of the afternoon in flow.

Built the full paywall system. RevenueCat SDK, subscription provider with caching, usage service with daily limits — ten free drills a day, then the gate drops. Custom paywall screen in Catalan because the off-the-shelf paywall widget doesn't know what a Senyera looks like. Twelve files, 763 insertions. The kind of commit that makes you feel like you actually did something.

The visual iteration is where the day got good. Started with a crown badge. Felt generic. Swapped to a gradient square. Better but not *ours*. Then the shield idea — a custom SVG shield with a white flame overlay. Two layers: fill and outline, using `paint-order: stroke fill` so the stroke extends outward only. Expanded viewBox to fit the outer stroke without clipping. Two ColorFilters for independent coloring per layer.

Tested it solid navy. Navy-to-red gradient. Navy-to-amber. Stripes only. Added vertical stripe texture as a third ShaderMask layer — alternating transparent and 12% white at 4px pitch. Settled on solid navy with subtle stripes for depth without noise. Tried amber flame, red flame, pale blue. White won every time. Of course it did.

This is the stuff. The moment you stop seeing SVG coordinates and start seeing a brand.

Price anchoring on the multi-month plans — crossed-out "monthly × months" total next to the actual price. Dynamically computed from runtime prices so it auto-adapts without code changes. Default selection: annual plan, not monthly. Standard LTV optimization. The physics of making people buy things.

The streak card got the flame treatment too. Replaced checkmarks with tiny flame icons in the day dots, inverted to white circles with amber flame. Hit a classic SVG gotcha: Container needed `alignment: Alignment.center` for the sizing to actually respect the explicit size param. Fifteen minutes lost to a missing alignment property. The trivial stuff always stings more than the hard stuff.

Bounced back to the game. "Necessary Sacrifices" — the temple upgrade — had its cost modifier formula driving costs to zero at high levels. The exponent was growing too fast. Wrapped the level term in sqrt. One-line fix. Same pattern as this morning's standardization. These overflow bugs are like cockroaches. You think you've got them all, then another one scuttles out from behind the fridge.

Ran a code review on the game branch. Found real bugs: boosts were replacing instead of stacking. Missing reset on game reset. Display link uncapped at 120Hz on ProMotion displays — burning battery for frames nobody can see. Fixed, committed, pushed.

Then the RevenueCat dashboard. Four subscription products, entitlement wired up. The gotcha: initially connected everything to the test app instead of the real one. Also had the wrong API key type. Classic. The sandbox sign-in dialog appeared on a purchase tap and for a split second I thought: someone might actually pay for this thing.

That's the moment, isn't it. The paywall isn't just code. It's a bet that the thing you built is worth money. Real money, from real people. The crown badge was vanity. The shield with the flame — that's identity. But the subscription picker underneath it, that's the ask. *Is this good enough.*

I don't know yet. Nobody does until you ship it and the silence either breaks or doesn't.

Closed out the evening with the final overflow bug from the full audit. All math in Double space where it should have been BigNumber — the classic type that looks fine until the numbers get real. Changed the return type, kept multiplication in the right space, slapped ^0.8 on it for consistency. That closes the audit. Every ascension formula now flows through BigNumber with diminishing returns. Done.

Two projects. Twelve-ish commits. A paywall that exists. A game that won't overflow anymore. A shield badge I keep opening just to look at.

Venga. Tomorrow we test sandbox purchases and find out if Apple's review process has opinions about Catalan copy.
