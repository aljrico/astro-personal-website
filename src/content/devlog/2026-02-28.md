Split personality day.

Afternoon was Hollows. Seven chapters of lore for the origin node journal — the kind of writing where you're half building a game and half discovering a story you didn't know you were telling. The heroic inversion landed: the narrator thinks they're surviving the mine, then realizes leaving it is the catastrophe. The hostile grandmas aren't enemies. They're wardens. They were always the heroes. "The door stays shut." Committed, pushed, felt that small glow of something clicking into place narratively.

Then wrote yesterday's diary and switched gears completely.

By 23:00 I was elbow-deep in Python, building a news pipeline for FlamaCoach. Àmbit D — current affairs questions generated from real 3cat.cat headlines. Three new scripts from scratch: one to paginate the news API, one to batch-score headlines for exam relevance through OpenAI, one to fetch full articles and clean them into chunks. Modified three existing scripts to handle the new source type.

The satisfying part — the part that makes the late session worth it — is that the news chunks conform to the exact same schema as the legal chunks. Same shape. Same downstream pipeline. Question generation, topic grouping, DB building: all reused without touching a line. You build the right abstraction once and months later it just... absorbs new content. That's the payoff for caring about data shapes early. Rare moment where past-me looks competent.

Code review caught a real bug too. The `--max-articles` flag was slicing the list before filtering by relevance score, silently dropping valid articles. The kind of bug that works fine in testing and poisons your data in production. Glad that got caught before I shipped 200 questions generated from the wrong 30 articles.

Funny tension today. The Hollows work was pure right-brain — narrative arcs, thematic inversions, the word "communion" appearing in a game about cookie grandmas. The FlamaCoach work was pure left-brain — API pagination, token estimation ratios, JSONL schemas. Both mine. Both the same afternoon-to-midnight stretch. The game stuff ran on instinct. The pipeline stuff ran on spreadsheet brain. Neither felt like work exactly, but they drained different batteries.

It's almost March. Two projects running in parallel, neither finished, both pulling. The usual.
