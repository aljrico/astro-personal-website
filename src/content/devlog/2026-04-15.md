Started the day wanting to profile the idle clicker on device. The universe said no. xctrace 16 and iOS 26 have a regression where it just sits there, frozen, waiting for the device to boot — device that is very much already booted. `devicectl` can see it fine. Instruments can't. CLI can't. Both affected. I spent twenty minutes debugging my debugging setup, which is a specific kind of hell.

Simulator it is.

Twenty seconds of Time Profiler and the top app-code hotspot jumps out immediately: `PremiumSkinIconView`. Thirteen percent CPU. Body alone: 253ms. Three separate calls to `currentSale` per render pass, each one reshuffling weighted candidates from scratch. And underneath all of it, `arePurchasesAvailable` quietly reading `totalClicks` and `totalWealth` on every single click — which in an idle clicker is, let's say, often.

I had a rough sense of what was wrong. Then I ran it by the AI assistant and it came back with the root cause sharper than I had it. Cleaner framing, tighter chain of causation. The invalidation path I'd sketched in my head was right, but I'd missed one link. It caught it. That's just the honest thing to say. Collaboration works better than ego.

We went spec-first this time before either of us touched code. That sounds obvious. It isn't always what happens when you're going fast. But this time we forced it: write down exactly what the cache keys are, what the invariants are, what the eviction policy is (none), what thread it runs on. Then implement. The spec took maybe fifteen minutes. The implementation was clean. No second-guessing, no "wait, what were we doing again."

Fix: memoize `currentSale` keyed by rotation index, month, and owned skins. Cache the full result. Also caught a latent bug while we were in there — `availability.start` was silently using call time instead of the rotation bucket start. Harmless because the only reader that mattered never triggered it. But it was wrong, and now it's right.

Post-fix: skin-icon cluster goes from 500ms to 12ms.

Ninety-seven percent.

I kept re-running the profiler just to watch the number. That's the stuff. The screen doing exactly what you knew it should do if you could just stop doing the stupid thing. Two hours of work and almost five hundred milliseconds of CPU time evaporates. Sometimes this job is genuinely good.

One small irritant: the profiling skill's trace parser doesn't resolve `<frame ref=>` pointers. About thirty percent of leaf nodes show up as "unknown." I worked around it inline with a one-off script but that's a known debt now. Worth fixing in the tool itself, not just in my workaround.

There are two more targets sitting in the notes. Decouple `arePurchasesAvailable` from `GameState` entirely — that breaks the invalidation chain for real, not just patches the symptom. And cache `DoublingUpgrade.cost` in the upgrade manager update loop, which is currently eating most of a 65ms frame. Both are more surgical than what we did today.

It's 6pm and the energy is different from this morning. Morning Alejandro was excited to profile. Evening Alejandro is glad it worked and slightly tired. The 97% win is real and I'm not going to let the tiredness take it from me. But the list of what's still left feels longer than it did at noon, and that's just how the day ends.

Tomorrow we'll pick up the chain.
