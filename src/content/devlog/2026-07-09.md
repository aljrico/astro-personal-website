Review tennis day. No new features. Just me and an external reviewer trading serves over release 1.8.0 all afternoon.

Round one: three findings. The P1 was the interesting kind of wrong. The reviewer painted a scary story about the XP flush losing data for players who never opted in. Traced it, and that path is unreachable: the pref check bails in microseconds while the alternative sits on a network await. But underneath the wrong story there was a real, smaller bug, a failed in-flight flush that quietly dropped a solve on retry. Fixed that. This keeps happening with these reviews. Wrong diagnosis, right smell. A doctor telling you it's the plague when it's a cold, but hey, at least he made you take your temperature.

The P2 was pure combat. Reviewer claimed the Android thermal listener never gets a baseline reading. So I went and read the actual AOSP source. ThermalManagerService posts the current status to every new listener the moment it registers. Flat-out wrong. Marked it false positive, moved on, felt smug for a good ten minutes.

Then the fun part. Killed some Material chevrons that broke my own icon rules and swapped in the house SVG, and turned the home ARENA button into a lone icon on a near-square pill that sizes itself off its sibling button. It looks right now. That's the entire acceptance criterion I have for UI: it looks right. Also discovered I can hot-reload the running app from a different session by firing SIGUSR1 at a flutter pid stashed in a scratchpad file. Deeply stupid. Works perfectly. I love it.

Round two came back after five. The reviewer confirmed two of three, and here's the thing: it was STILL wrong about the thermal baseline. I checked the source again. It posts. But the behavior is undocumented, and I've been burned by "undocumented but true" before, so I hardened it anyway. Explicit baseline callback before registration, a dedup for the duplicate. Winning the argument and writing the defensive code anyway. That's not losing. That's a peace treaty.

The scare of the evening: ran the duel screen tests and got 16 failures. Hostia. Fifteen of them turned out to be the same bug wearing different hats, a bootstrap in initState reaching for the locale before the widget is allowed to look. Pre-existing on the branch, not from anything I touched this afternoon. I checked twice, because the impostor voice was already sure it was mine. Moved the bootstrap one lifecycle stop later and all fifteen fell over together. The last one was a test complaining about zone labels I'd deliberately shouted into uppercase weeks ago. The test learned to shout too.

133 out of 133 green. Everything pushed.

Nothing to show anyone today. No screenshot, no new toy. Just a release branch that's a little more honest than it was this morning. Some days you paint. Some days you scrape the hull.
