Plumbing day. Two projects, no new features, nothing to show anyone. Just making the existing stuff work better. There's a version of me that finds this deeply satisfying and another version that wonders if I'm hiding in maintenance to avoid the harder creative decisions. Both are probably right.

The FlamaCoach pipeline got a full end-to-end run. 18,000 headlines from 3cat, filtered down to 809 worth turning into exam questions, which produced 1,953 questions. The numbers feel good but the real work was upstream — the categorization was broken. Everything that didn't fit neatly into a topic was landing in "Uncategorized," which is the data engineering equivalent of shoving things under the bed. Fixed it by regenerating the drill-topics with proper bloc definitions, then built a grouping mechanism to merge 19 noisy upstream categories into 8 clean thematic ones. Internacional, Política Espanyola, Economia i Ciència — legible, human, the kind of labels that make sense when you're studying at 2am.

64 topics. 12,741 questions. Rebuilt the DB, committed, pushed. Done.

The thing about pipeline work is that it *feels* like programming but it's really just plumbing. You're connecting pipes, adjusting flow rates, making sure the water comes out clean on the other end. It's satisfying the way organizing a closet is satisfying — everything in its place, nothing wasted. But you don't tell anyone about it. Nobody's excited about your closet.

Switched to the clicker. Found a bug that's been silently leaking wealth after ascension. The kind of bug that makes you feel stupid for not catching it sooner and simultaneously impressed that the game worked at all. Race condition: `BuildingsManager.reset()` was clearing everything *except* the cached `buildingsProduction` stored property. So the first game tick after unPause used stale pre-ascension production numbers. You ascend, your wealth is supposed to zero, and for one tick — just one — the old production sneaks through. Same bug existed in the transcendence path too.

A subagent traced the full ascension flow and spotted the deferred Task race. I love that — having a tireless second pair of eyes that will actually read every line of a 200-line flow without getting bored at line 40. The fix was three lines. Zero the cache eagerly in reset, belt-and-suspenders zeroing in both ascend paths. Five minutes of typing, two hours of understanding.

Code review of the Hollows commits turned up one real issue: the typewriter animation in HollowsChestSheet could orphan Tasks if `.onAppear` fired multiple times. Cancel the previous task before starting a new one. One line. The kind of bug you only notice when you go looking, and the kind of fix that makes you wonder how many other one-liners are hiding in plain sight.

Ended the day removing a placeholder string from FlamaCoach topic rows. "Contingut pendent de descripció." The most meaningless text in the app, displayed proudly to every user. Topics with no description now just... don't show a description. Revolutionary.

March. New month. Didn't even notice the date change until I wrote it down. The Hollows narrative work from last week still feels like the most interesting thing I've done in a while, and today felt like the comedown after. Necessary work. Good work. But the editor stayed in maintenance mode all afternoon and part of me is already scanning for what's next.
