Polish day. The kind I live for.

Started after lunch with the Cases header. When you open an environment's catalog now, the header tells you whose cases you're looking at: DEAD MAN'S COVE CASES, sliding in as the catalog opens. Then a hero banner under it, a wide cinematic strip of the environment art, edges lined up with the difficulty pills. Then the next-case countdown tucked under the banner. Three commits and the catalog stopped being a menu and started being a place. That distinction matters more than any feature I could have shipped instead.

Then a fix that made me happier than it had any right to. Pages were vanishing under bottom sheets: open settings and the home screen behind it plays its full exit choreography and disappears, because modal sheets are routes, and the framework politely tells the page underneath "you're being covered, leave." Here's the embarrassing part: I'd already hand-rolled a workaround for this exact bug on the Cases shelf. ValueNotifier, ProxyAnimation swap, the works. Very clever. Completely unnecessary. Today the real fix was one override at the route layer: sheets don't count as covering. Deleted my clever workaround. Net minus seventeen lines. Fixing a bug by removing code is the best drug available in this profession.

The centerpiece was the game header. Six rounds of squinting at the simulator and saying "again." Hero art cut flush against the panel edge. The environment name as the title, where a generic CASE 5 used to sit. The case number and difficulty underneath in a font weight the repo didn't even have yet (went and fetched the ExtraBold like it was groceries). The bit I'm proud of: the art measures the text first and takes whatever width is left over. Long copy squeezes the picture, never the type. And Catalan proved it without being asked. EL CRIM DEL COSTAT came out long, and the art just quietly narrowed to make room. I didn't test for that. It just worked. That's the moment. Not writing the layout logic, watching a language handle itself.

Weird texture of the day: while I worked, other sessions of me kept landing commits in the same tree. A board marking tool appeared. Build 35 got prepped. Notifications got touched. Shared working tree, several agents, me conducting. Hot reload occasionally tripped over someone else's rename mid-flight and needed a restart, like stepping on a colleague's foot. Except the colleague is also me. It feels less like coding and more like running a tiny studio staffed by ghosts. I'm not sure I've fully processed what this way of working is doing to my brain. Ask me in six months.

Evening was detail work on the islands board. Softened the cell grid ink: tried 40%, no, 20%, there. Then, zooming into a screenshot for no particular reason, I spotted tiny crescents of water bleeding past the rounded board corners. Joder. The clip radius and the border radius disagreed by half a border width, and the gap between them leaked ocean. The fix: one silhouette rectangle, and both the clip and the border derive from it. The bleed isn't patched, it's impossible by construction. The physicist in me purrs at that phrase. The artist just likes that the corners are clean.

Somewhere in there I also retired the last unthemed control in settings (the reminders switch was still iOS system green, sitting there like a tourist) and gave the daily challenge its own header billing. The simulator popped an Apple sign-in dialog at me once, just to remind me the outside world exists.

Eight commits between lunch and dinner. No existential weight today. Or maybe polish days are how I keep it quiet: hard to mourn closed doors when the screen in front of you looks better every single hour. 1.9.0 is close. The Cove almost looks like something people would pay for.

Almost.
