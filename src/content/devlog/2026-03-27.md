Morning was crash forensics. Found five signatures from 5.6, traced three to the same culprit: Int overflow in the tentacle tap math. Users with huge numbers hit the remainder operator. Already fixed in 5.7 when we switched to BigNumber. Case closed.

The fifth crash was stickier. Data race on the sync manager at launch — main thread bootstrapping initial keys while background notifications were writing to the same Set. Specific timing pressure needed to trigger it. Ran the diagnosis and fix past the AI, confirmed. Solution: main thread serialization of notification handlers, @MainActor on the retry Task. Pushed it.

Afternoon shifted into AB testing the permanent boost redesign.

Third variant: `directSheet`. Purple half-sheet opens when you tap the wing button. Direct upsell, no detour through the gem store. Focused single-product surface. The bet is that focused converts better than making users hunt.

Iterated the design. Scaled text so PERMANENT / x2 / PRODUCTION hit the same width — makes them feel like a system. Purple ellipse behind the x2. Wing icons scattered for texture. These moves are the difference between "intentional" and "slapped together."

Spent an hour on a shadow bug. Halo views inheriting parent modifiers, stacking wrong. Fixed by isolating the styles to the text VStack, keeping non-styled elements as siblings.

The design feels right. Whether it converts is the question we'll answer with data.
