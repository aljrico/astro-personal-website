Spent the afternoon in Xcode Organizer forensics. Five crash signatures from v5.6, all begging to be explained. Three of them pointed to the same place: Int(hugeFiniteDouble) in splitAmount's remainder math. Int overflow, not NaN. Not subtle — direct trap. The kind that makes you feel better when you see it, because at least it's *clear*. These were dead code walking from before the BigNumber migration. We already fixed it in 5.7. Validation: good.

Crash #5 was messier. Launch EXC_BAD_ACCESS on CloudSyncManager. Data race between the main thread bootstrap and background iCloud notification handlers. Classic pattern — you initialize on one thread, a notification fires on another, both reach for the same Set at the same time. The fix was obvious once I saw it: serialize the notification handlers to main thread, wrap the retry Task in @MainActor. Simple. Ship it.

Called in external analysis (gpt-5.4, reasoning mode) to sanity-check the Int overflow diagnosis. Wanted confirmation I wasn't missing something. Got it. That validation cost thirty seconds and probably saved me six months of doubt. Not a bad tradeoff.

By evening, the crash work felt complete enough. Committed and pushed. Then the energy shifted.

Started on a third AB variant for the boost redesign — directSheet. The idea: skip the gem store entirely. Tap the purple wing button, get a half-sheet selling permanent boost directly. Focused surface. Fewer choices. Conversion should follow.

Spent two hours on the polish. Scaled text trick to match the widths of PERMANENT, x2, PRODUCTION. Purple ellipse behind the multiplier. Wing icons scattered for visual rhythm. The thing clicked together around 7pm. That's the moment — the screen does what you pictured. Showed it to everyone in the house. Nobody was as excited as the code deserved but that's fine. I was enough.

Found a SwiftUI shadow bug along the way. Halo views were inheriting parent modifiers they shouldn't have — shadow, foreground color, the works. Moved the styling to the text VStack and kept sibling elements unstyled. Small fix. Took twenty minutes to find because I kept looking in the wrong place. But it's fixed.

Two registers today. The detective work paid for itself — crash triage is unsexy but necessary. The feature work paid for the soul — that's where the energy lives. Need both. Can't build momentum only on bugs. Can't ship bugs only on momentum.

Tomorrow: Push the directSheet through QA. See if the hypothesis holds.
