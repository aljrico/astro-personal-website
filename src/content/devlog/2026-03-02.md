Late night. Big push. The official exam system — eight convocatories with metadata, scoring formula, attempt tracking, the whole thing — went out in one commit. The kind of session where you look up and it's half ten and you've built a complete feature without pausing. Selection screen with places, aspirants, time limits. Results screen with APTE / NO APTE. The scoring formula is real: negative marking, pass at 4.0, the works.

There's something grounding about implementing a formula that thousands of people's careers depend on. `P = (C - I×0.25) / N × 10`. Get it right.

Fifteen minutes later I'm reading my own diff and — hostia. The finish method was mutating state, adding the last answer to the results list, then building the persisted score from that same list. Which now had N+1 elements. Last answer counted twice.

Flutter analyze? Blind to it. The linter doesn't know you just mutated `state` and are now reading derived values from the new version while your brain is still in the old one. Only eyes catch that. Mine almost didn't.

Fixed it in ten minutes. Two lines. The kind of bug that would've shipped and showed scores slightly off — not enough for anyone to notice immediately, just enough to slowly rot trust. The worst kind. Worth tattooing somewhere: in any Notifier method that mutates state and then reads from it, you're holding a loaded gun. Your mental model says "I'm still in the old world." You're not.

Morning. Different energy. Polish mode.

The exam cards had metadata in the wrong order. Questions and time limit should come before places and aspirants. Format info first, context second. Small thing. Obvious once you see it. Invisible until you do.

Needed a separator for the pass ratio. Tried an arrow first. Too dramatic. Middot won. These decisions take thirty seconds and matter more than they should. But this is where the flow lives — the moment the spacing clicks, the weight of bold text against regular, the thing that makes it feel *considered* instead of generated.

Removed the repeat button from results. One button left, so the wrapping Column became noise. Unwrapped it. The kind of cleanup that makes future-me think past-me was tidy. He wasn't. He just got annoyed by unnecessary nesting.

Three commits. One big, one fix, one polish. The exam feature is real now. Eight past convocatories. Real conditions. Real scoring. APTE or NO APTE.

Noon. The evaluation screen was still showing per-question feedback — correct/wrong highlighting, the answer footer, the whole pedagogical apparatus. Wrong mode. AVALUA'T is supposed to feel like a real exam. No hand-holding. You pick, it moves on. 250ms pause so you feel the weight of your choice, then next question. Mirrored the pattern from the official exam notifier. Killed the next() method, ripped out the footer animation controller. Net negative 21 lines. The screen breathes now.

Then the progress bar. Swapped the "Pregunta X de Y" text for an actual animated bar. Wrapped the fill in a TweenAnimationBuilder — 350ms, easeInOut. Here's the thing I'm proud of: the animation lives at the widget level, not the call site. One change, four screens get smooth transitions for free. Drill, evaluation, simulacre, topics. All of them. That's the kind of decision that costs nothing now and pays compound interest.

Afternoon. Two codebases open, two branches, the brain doing context switches every twenty minutes. The iOS app needed a daily rewards card. Horizontal scroll, reward tiles, calendar icon with a streak overlay. The feature itself was straightforward. The colors weren't.

Caramel. Minty. Mango. Watermelon. MidBlue. I went through all of them. Spent longer picking colors than building the scroll view. Settled on two-tone classicBlue — header and tiles unified, white base. Sometimes you iterate through five wrong answers to recognize the right one. Sometimes the right one was the most boring option all along.

Then the pixel fussing. Rounder tile corners, 0.25 to 0.32 of the width. Vertical padding fix because today's scaled tile was clipping at the edges. Shadow tweaked to match existing cards. Added a subtitle — "Come back every day for better rewards!" — and iterated the font size through 0.45, 0.47, 0.49, 0.51, 0.54. Five builds to move a number three points. This is either craftsmanship or insanity. Probably both.

Extended the scroll range while I was in there. Used to show streak minus three to streak plus five. Now it goes all the way back to day one and ten days into the future. Full history. The auto-scroll to today was already in place so the UX didn't change — just more room to explore.

Last touch on rewards: moved skins from every 10 days to every 28, replaced the old slot with fortune wheel spins that scale as the square root of the day. Day 10 gets you 3 spins, day 100 gets you 10. The physicist brain wanted a logarithmic curve. The game designer brain wanted something that felt generous early and still rewarding late. Square root split the difference.
