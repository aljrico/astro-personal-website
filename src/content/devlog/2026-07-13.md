One in the morning and I'm punching a lowercase "i" out of a badge.

The info button needed an icon. Drew one by hand as SVG first — not faithful, looked like a tourist sign. So I pasted the reference PNG through the tracing pipeline instead, and the final version is the good kind of dumb: a solid chocolate-red badge with the glyph cut out as actual holes, so whatever's behind it shows through the letter. Transparent where it matters. I liked it more than the feature itself, which currently does nothing when you tap it. A button with no action. Pure potential.

Then twenty minutes lost to a lie. Edited the SVG, hot reloaded, nothing changed. Edited again. Nothing. Questioned the tracer, questioned the file, questioned myself. Turns out flutter_svg caches parsed assets by key — plain reload silently serves the stale art. The whole time the fix was working and the app was just showing me the past. Joder. Hot restart, there it was, perfect on the first try from ten minutes ago.

Slept. Woke up. Put on the physicist hat.

Afternoon was numbers, and the numbers came to collect. I finally got the real per-country AdMob revenue export instead of estimating from eCPM, and my model had been flattering me by 9 to 24 percent. Of course it had. Estimates always err in your favor — not because the math is biased, because you are. The US was doubly flattered: stale eCPM *and* AdMob attributes way fewer US impressions than my own events claim. The US premium over Spain dropped from 4.3x to 2.5x just by looking at reality. Germany turns out to be a quiet sleeper. Russia is exactly $0.00 — not low, zero, AdMob simply doesn't monetize there. I'd been assigning value to users who are, financially speaking, ghosts.

Blended D30 LTV: eleven cents. The average player gives me about four days of their life and I earn eleven cents for it.

One genuinely good number though: the D7 retention panic from last week was noise. The scary refit was built on 169 users. With almost six thousand, D7 sits at 12.1%, right where the original curve said it would. I re-derived my own conclusions and the earlier me was wrong for boring statistical reasons. Small samples will make you feel things you don't need to feel.

Evening was the payoff. The game screen has been sitting on a flat dead background for months while the home screen behind it had this living animated sky, and tonight it finally bled through. Two culprits, stacked. Fixed the opaque surface layer — still black. Hostia. Turned out the case-entry loader painted an ink base that *never left the widget stack*. It had been sitting there, opaque, behind every game you've ever played. Now it dissolves with the reveal and the whole screen breathes.

Undo shipped too. The implementation is proudly stupid: a bounded stack of a hundred full board snapshots. No deltas, no command pattern, no cleverness. Snapshot, restore, done. Somewhere a cleaner architecture exists and I don't care. It works, it's atomic, ship it.

The whole session was that loop I've stopped being able to live without — describe the change, watch the simulator blink, adjust, again. Buttons converging on the same ghost-cream square style one reload at a time. Flow, the visual kind, the only kind I get.

Then the tax. The working tree was carrying 2.4 thousand lines of a *different* session's island work, tangled into the same fifteen translation files. Spent the tail of the night with tweezers, staging individual hunks by key, verifying the extracted commit alone in a temp worktree like a paranoid surgeon. An hour of pure grind for a clean history nobody will ever read.

Pushed it. The release branch is getting fat — chrome, undo, the ledger, islands still brewing on the side. 1.9.0 is turning into one of *those* releases.

Eleven cents per player. But the sky dissolves beautifully now, and I keep opening the game just to watch it happen.
