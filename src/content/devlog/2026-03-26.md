The Flutter game got a face today. Not a good face yet — but a face. Ripped out every Material icon and replaced them with actual art from the Rhos pack. Currency badges, nav buttons, upgrade rows. The kind of work that doesn't show up in any metric but changes how the whole thing *feels* when you tap around. Went from "dev placeholder" to "someone cared about this."

Upgrade panel went through four or five layouts. Two columns, three columns, aspect ratios bouncing between 1.8 and 3.5. Name on the left, stats on the right, cost pill below. Then name on top, cost below. Then back. The iteration was fast — almost too fast. Each version looked fine for thirty seconds until I noticed something off. Landed on three columns with a name-left / stats-right split that finally breathes. The trick was showing actual stat values instead of just the increment. Level 0 damage showing 1.0 instead of 0.0. Small thing. Makes the whole panel feel like information instead of decoration.

Polygon envelopes for enemy shapes was the fun detour. Modulating base radius with cosine so triangles look triangular and hexagons look hexagonal. Added random spin per enemy. The wobble reads through the silhouette now. This is the stuff — fifteen minutes of math and suddenly the enemies have personality.

Crit projectiles: killed the gold color. Same red blob but 2x size. Size over color. When your blob is already pink-red, a gold flash reads as a different entity, not a stronger hit. Bigger blob = bigger hit. Obvious in retrospect. Took longer to decide than to implement.

Then the rename.

Cash → Coins. Coins → BlueStars. BlueStars → Blood. Sixteen files, two phases to avoid collision. The kind of mechanical refactoring that sounds trivial until you're three layers deep in a find-replace and realize your JSON save keys need backward-compat migration. But the real work wasn't the code — it was the identity decision. The permanent currency is now "Blood," visualized as a red potion drop. Extracted the exact scarlet from the icon with Pillow (E62E49) and pushed it through every downstream visual. Blobs, projectiles, fusion glow, buttons, HP gradients. One hex value and the whole game shifted from watermelon pink to something that actually matches what you're collecting.

Font exploration was the rabbit hole I didn't plan. Tried KG Happy Solid, Barlow Semi Condensed, landed on Lilita One as the global font. Set it once in ThemeData, extracted to a constant, deleted every hardcoded reference. The game looks like a game now instead of a prototype wearing a system font.

Tilted checkerboard background on the game field. 56px cells, 10-degree rotation, alternating grays. Tried 45 degrees first — too aggressive, looked like a chess board having a seizure. Ten degrees is subtle enough that you don't notice it consciously but the field stops feeling flat.

---

Switched to the iOS app in the evening. The LTV investigation landed its verdict and it's not pretty.

Version 5.0's gem store redesign killed Piggy Bank conversion. Five times over. From 3.1% to 0.6%. And the thing that stings — it was never AB tested. Just shipped. Seven subsequent versions and nobody noticed the conversion stayed suppressed. The redesign was the sole cause. Not a gradual decline, not market conditions, not seasonality. One UI change, one metric cratered, eight months of lost revenue.

Built an AB test for a new PB card design — bold x2 hero, scattered wings, the whole thing. If the treatment wins, we claw some of it back. If it doesn't, at least we'll know the old layout wasn't the only factor.

The forensic work was satisfying in the way that debugging always is — hours of noise collapsing into one clean signal. But there's a lesson underneath that I keep learning and keep forgetting: ship fast, yes, but AB test the things that touch money. The five minutes it takes to wire up an experiment could have saved months of silent bleeding.

Twelve hours. Two projects. One got prettier, one got diagnosed. Neither is finished. The usual.
