The whole in-game entrance runs on one timeline now. One controller, 1700 milliseconds, and everything hangs off it. The floorplan fades in, then the walls, then the victim pops onto the board, then the furniture staggers in one piece at a time, the tool tray, the suspect buttons with their little icons popping after. Then the clue types itself out at the end like an old typewriter.

Before today it was a mess of separate controllers all firing on their own clocks. Each widget animating in its own little universe, none of them aware of the others. Now it's a single conductor. The board art doesn't even own a controller anymore — it just asks the scope what time it is.

When it sequenced into place for the first time I sat there triggering it over and over. Hot restart, watch. Hot restart, watch. This is the stuff. The moment the screen does exactly the thing you pictured in your head. "Fucking perfect," I said out loud. To no one.

Then the typewriter lied to me.

First run, the clue appeared instantly — full sentence, no typing. I assumed staleness, some rebuild swallowing the animation, and spent a minute chasing that ghost. Wasn't staleness at all. The selected-suspect provider defaults to the victim's id, so the panel was rendering the victim's *statement* — rich styled spans — not the plain prompt I thought I was animating. The typewriter was dumping the whole thing instantly because it had no idea how to walk through styled text. Hostia. So I taught it: reveal the spans character by character, truncate while preserving the styles, count a widget span as one. Reserve the final size underneath with an invisible placeholder so the layout doesn't jump. Only type on the live entrance — switch suspects later and it just shows the full text, no theatrics.

That kind of bug is annoying for exactly as long as it takes to understand it. The moment you understand it, the fix is obvious and clean. Those are the good ones.

The second half of the night was less glamorous and more important. I handed the whole diff to a subagent and told it to tear the thing apart, max rigor, find what I missed. And it found something that made my stomach drop. The entire UI's visibility was gated on the board images loading *successfully*. If that future errored — a hiccup, a missing asset, anything — the controller would never start, and every fade and pop would sit frozen at opacity zero. A blank, unplayable game. The polish had quietly built a single point of failure where there hadn't been one. Beautiful animations are a great place to hide a landmine.

Fixed it the boring correct way: start the entrance on success *or* error, and on error snap everything straight to its resting state so it all renders. Then cleaned up the leaks the review flagged — a couple of wrappers were rebuilding their curves every frame, each one quietly leaking a listener onto the shared controller. Swapped them for the leak-free version. Deleted dead code, named the magic numbers. The tidying you do once the fun is over.

Pushed both. The choreography and the hardening.

Funny how the day split. The first half was pure play — making something look right, watching it, grinning like an idiot. The second half was me, through a proxy, coming in to point out that the pretty thing was one failed network call away from a black screen. Both halves were the job. Only one of them felt like it.

It's late. The entrance still looks fucking perfect. I keep triggering it.
